About my Doctor Who fics:

When I first started watching Doctor Who (my first story was Dragonfire), I noticed sexual tension between characters right from the start. As I got hooked on it and kept watching, I picked up on more of the tension, as well as innuendo, funny stuff, and got attached to various characters. The ones I became attached to first were the four Iceworld Soldiers in Dragonfire (they are the most intreiging too since they only have one story). I also became quite a Doctor Who romantic, even trying to make up 'shipping names (ie. Renegadeshipping). I've also always been the minority in fandom, blatenty loving Adric, Mel, the Seventh Doctor, The Sixth Doctor, the Anthony Ainley Master, and the infamous Doctor in Distress song.

Well, as you can see, this all becomes apparent in my work. Some of my fics are Askew (Who Askew being a name I came up with after watching one too many Kevin Smith films :) ), others are borderline Askew, and some are *gasp* serious. But what makes a fic (Who) Askew? Well, it usually contains much of the stuff mentioned in the above paragraph. But Askew stuff is more than sex, oh no. It's about the dark side of fandom (and also breaking the fourth wall). It's about various Doctors, Companions, and Villians searching for acceptance. It's about relationships that bounce about like crazy. It's about four lone souls, brought back from the dead, wondering what they're supposed to do now. And it's about the Seventh Doctor tormented because of the one girl he didn't save.

So, gentle reader, I invite you to laugh, cry, groan, and enjoy. Some scenes may fall flat on their faces, but not to worry, to me it's all good. So grab a bag of Jelly Babies, and read this crazy shit.

After all, it's better than what the BBC pumps out these days!

-Astrid
