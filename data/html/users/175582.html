Author has written 1 story for Final Fantasy VIII.

It was a boring day at work when I decided to check reviews for my old old story on fanfiction.net, and I was so moved by all the reviews that I felt I had a responsibility to let my readers know what will be happening to the story. As a fan who silently lurks the pages of fanfiction.net, I too get riled up when I find an unfinished story lying around like an orphan. Well, I won't be that parent who drops him off at the church in a box; I will be the parent who comes back, more matured and in a better financial position,and gives the child the home he deserves.

Please note that this author has yet to achieve that better financial position.

I still dearly dearly love Quistis and Seifer, but unfortunately they're not my current obsession. (I have fallen victim to the dashing Sean Bean) My writing skills have improved, but a year of writing research papers have changed my style of writing and I find that I can no longer make my stories flow the way Very Different People have. I'm still very proud of the story, because I can truthfully say that I thought up the Seifer-comes-back-under-Quistis'-responsibility idea. These stories are rampant now, but back in the days (those good ol' days) there were only a few well respected authors and I aspired to be one of them (mission unaccomplished).

So basically, if you peel back the not-so-witty side remarks and stale allegories, what I'm trying to say is, this story will no longer be updated. I am tempted to rewrite it, but again, seeing how the plot is no longer unique, the temptation is only a really bad itch. Although, if I ever have extra time on my hands, I will definitely use this as an oppurtunity to polish my writing skills and rewrite the story. Hopefully by the time revisions for Very Different People gets up, there will still be readers who find it enjoyable and recognize it as That-Entertaining-Little-Thing-That-Author-Dropped-Cold-Turket-Four-Years-Ago. A great big hug to all those who find my story a good tickle - I write for you. Please don't hesitate to email me at sakui@bu.edu, but state in the subject line that you're from ff.net, or I might think you're a clever spammer.

Buon giorno
