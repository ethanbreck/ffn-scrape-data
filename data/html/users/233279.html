So a little about me. I am a 34-year-old college student that still lives at home with Mom and Grandmother and has no intention of moving on. I have a wide variety of tastes in music, books, movies, and comics.

I am a supporter of gay rights, individualism, the NRA and strict gun control, religion being freely practiced no matter what it is, peace, and the Military. I am a patriot, but distrust the government. I voted for Obama, but I am waiting to see if I made the right choice.

I own five dogs, 3 birds, and a host of imaginary friends. I spend all of my time at work, home, and on the internet, while occasionally ghosting through the library.

I have four brothers and one sister, that I know of, and only see my sister. She has blessed me with a wonderful brother-in-law, so I can actually claim to have five brothers. I also have a sweet nephew and a niece who are rapidly becoming independent and wanting to do things their way, which I believe is the family motto.

I am a writer of fanfiction and original fiction, mostly slash and gen. I have an embarrassing habit of writing teenage fiction when I do original work. I'm an avid reader and writer of fanfiction and absolutely obsessed with four very cool brunette's. Xander Harris, Joxer, Harry Potter and of course Wesley Wyndam-Price. I mostly post my work on Twisting the Hellmouth and my own site Beyond Canon now. I have fallen in love with the writings of Retirw her Mag 7 fic is wonderful. I'm a perpetual WIP Queen and broke college student, who works as a clerk at a hotel. I am a romantic, with a realist’s vision that the world will usually kick you in the teeth given the chance, but that it is no reason to give up hope.

I believe that the United States is not the land of plenty for everyone and that there are people who must choose between medicine and food; I am and have been one of those people. I believe that so called economic stimulus plans are just a smoke screen, because if you look at those plans very few will qualify.

Yet, I love my country and know that I am lucky to have been born in the US, where despite our problems I still have the right to say this, piss people off, and dress anyway I want instead of worry about what will be done to me because I am a woman.

I am strange and eccentric and have an obsession with chocolate, even though I am allergic. In short, I’m your average American girl.
