Once more, I have decided to edit my bio. In actuality, it seems rather pointless. We write meaningless sayings that only 1/10th of the population of readers actually glance at only to receive no feedback in return, except on those quotes that you stole from someone else. Like I said-pointless, but we do it anyway. We write and write and write hoping to inform the reader of what we are like in actuality, but they never seem to connect to our outer worldly way of thinking, which actually sucks ‘cause that means we wrote for hours on end for no damn reason. Although, I have nothing to complain about. This paragraph has only taken me one minute to write.

I would give you a list of things I like or a list of reasons why I don’t update often, but that seems rather pointless. You don’t care what I like, and if you did, you would know what I like by the time that you’ve read my fanfiction, which is pretty much the only reason you would come here in the first place. Are you getting the point that I don’t know why the hell my fingers are still typing words? I do. Let me try to explain myself in the longest way possible…

Hi. I’m me.

Okay… that was pretty short. If you want an elongated explanation….

Hi. The name on my birth certificate says that my name is Haviland Nicole , but I don’t know if I truly believe that underlined part. Actually, I didn’t underline; moreover, my ‘father’ told me that I wasn’t allowed to post my name on the Internet. Yeah, right. He has his work’s site named for our last name. How stupid is that, huh? Anyways… People call me ‘Haviland,’ ‘Havie,’ ‘Ase,’ and many other names that I don’t particularly wish to write down on a site read by five-year olds. It should come as no surprise that I like to write, read, and talk about fanfiction. Supposedly, I update my fanfiction once every month when I actually decide to dump the Sims or Everquest to actually get off my upstairs computer and walk to my downstairs one. Then I have to type for hours on end, constantly editing and reediting what I’ve written. Some writers start at the beginning and work from there on. Some start at the middle and work outwards. I, however, start at the end then go to the beginning and then move to the middle to actually do something. Don’t I have a complicated writing schedule?

One major thing I have a problem with is my memory. It and I don’t often agree with one another. My parents also tend to disagree with my memory. For instance, my memory though that I broke my arm when I was three years old, jumping off the couch onto a chair and falling onto my arm. It turns out that I broke it when I was two by jumping off a stool about a foot high and falling onto the floor, arm first. We had a thirty minute discussion on how I went to the doctor and screamed bloody murder since it was only have broken and he had to break it more. I have never forgiven him for breaking my arm, and he has never forgiven me for kicking him in the balls. Then it turns out, I did forgive him for breaking my arm, and I never even kicked him in the balls. So that means that he just hasn’t forgiven me for being alive. I swear.

My memory also does this weird thing where if I memorize a vocabulary word by writing it and it’s definition down ten times, then the only way that I can remember the definition is if I write the word, which will then conclude in me subconsciously writing down the definition. (Somehow, Microsoft Word just said that that was a complete sentence.)

If you have actually decided to read this and the previous paragraphs, then the conclusion can come that you are completely and utterly insane for even trying to understand a semblance of a mind that can’t even comprehend itself. I though I was sane. It turns out that the monkeys weren’t ever there and that aliens aren’t out to get me. I’m mad at aliens. One boy in my class said that aliens are stupid. I replied, “Which ones? Extra terrestrials or Mexicans?” Everyone laughed and didn’t answer my question. I truly didn’t know which ones he was saying were stupid. I swear.

My friends send me quizzes often. You know… the ones where they answer question and send it to you so that you can answer the questions and send it back to them. Well, anyways… she sent me this quiz that had an odd question on it: If E.T. rang your doorbell, holding a peace sign and asking if he could use your phone, would you let him? My response was simple: No way. How does he know what a peace sign is? For him, a peace sign could be an uzi or a rocket launcher. If he came to my door, then I’d grab my baseball bat and bash his brains in. Anyone want extra terrestrial kidneys for supper? My friends haven’t sent me any more questions like that one.

I think that the miniseries, ‘Taken,’ is one of the greatest ever done. The main reason I think this is because of how the plot is so delicately woven throughout the generations of these three families. All of them are driven through happiness, grief, and insanity before ending up in a state of tranquil acceptance where they all just look to the sky and decide to play their games beneath it instead of trying to interfere with it. Also, the quotes said and made by a little girl just like the character she’s acting give you chills throughout your body every time they are said. One of my favorite quotes is from the second episode. It says…

“You know in cartoons the way someone can run off a cliff and they’re fine. They don’t fall… until they look down. My mom always said that was the secret to life: never look down. But it’s not just that. It’s not just about not looking. It’s about not ever realizing that you’re in the middle of the air and that you don’t know how to fly.”

There are many other quotes. More memorable. More chilling. But that one was my favorite. Because I’ve realized that I don’t know anything and that I can’t do anything about my ignorance- I’ll always be this way. I guess that’s what has set me free in life. I know that no matter the constrictions, no matter what I like or don’t, there’s just nothing that I will never be able to do. I’m not in charge. Neither are all the grown-ups that I adore so much. They know just as much as I do about the questions that really matter.

There is something out there. A higher power, a god, another race, I really don’t know what. All I know is that there is something out there, waiting for us. Whether they know it or not, we’ll come together one day. We’ll see what we were wanting to. We’ll understand. Until then, we’re just along for the ride.

I guess that ends my meaningful soliloquy. I’ve said what I’ve wanted to. More so then I thought I would. I’m not anything special. I’ve just given up on trying to be. If somehow I get fame, then I get it. I won’t want it until then. I won’t try and strive to get there just to feel it. I don’t know how it’s so much different- being famous and being unknown. Either way… you feel alone until you have someone there. Either way… everyone’s out to get you. Either way… you’re alive.
