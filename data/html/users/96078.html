A note to readers:

I joined fanfiction.net in 2001, and until about 2004 I wrote continuously and prolifically. In 2005 I left for college to become a creative writing major, but quickly changed to a major in vocal performance and musicology. While music is an all-consuming lifestyle (as well as past time) I have not forgotton about my love for writing - strangely, my particular love for writing fanfictions. I am now a junior at Bard College in Annandale-on-Hudson, New York, and am ready once again to pursue my love for storytelling. I have erased all of my old stories which were incomplete to begin on a clean slate, but have left the one relic of my highschool writing career - the story "Bound." While the writing is practically infantile, I still feel as though it is a great accomplishment (my commitment to finishing stories has often been lacking) and will therefore never take it down. However, I am thinking that I will now begin anew: I am working on a brand new fanfiction, Harry Potter of course, spoilers books 1 - 7. I'm excited to become a part of fanfiction.net again!

About me:

My life is pretty much my major. Everything I do revolves around Opera and music from the 19th century and earlier (though I specialize in early opera and Mozart). I'm possibly the most ridiculous person you'll ever meet. I'm also possibly the biggest Harry Potter fanatic alive (no joke, ask anyone). I have very little modesty, and that trait displays itself wherever I go - it's not something that you won't notice about me. I love Urban Exploration. I was born in October and every birthday party I ever had was centered around horror (because of halloween). Because of this, I have always had a fascination with the macabre. I enjoy everything scary. I am a big fan of body modification - I went a little nuts in highschool/freshman year of college and pierced a lot of different things. Most of the piercings are out now, but several of my favorites still survive. I also have 5 tattoos, many of them music related, and all rest highly significant and not at all kitschy. Anything else that interests you, feel free to ask. I'm an open book.
