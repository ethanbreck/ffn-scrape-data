What's next for Dani Reese and Charlie Crews?
1 Cell No. Six: Reese gets a letter from a detective who used to work undercover with her. He now works with the department up in Crescent City, California, home of Pelican Bay State Prison. He writes that someone has been trying to kill him and that it has something to do with Crews. He says he can't trust anyone but her and is coming down to talk to her. That man, Detective Patrick Ballantine, later winds up dead...in her apartment. He's holding a note with the words Cell No. Six written on them. Crews knows what that is and he's not happy about it.
2 The Headless Horseman of Los Alamitos: A jockey's headless body tears up the Los Alamitos Race Course the day before a big race. So, where's his head? Check the trophy case, Sherlock. Just who made a prize out of Marco Duponte...and was it just about the race?
3 Breaking the Code: When a mathematician turns up dead at in one of the classrooms at the California Institute of Technology, Crews and Reese have exactly fifteen and a half hours to solve one equation before a theory renders the Los Angeles water supply vaporized. Their dead mathematician has all the answers, or does he?
4 Starfish Express: Construction of a new water park near Santa Monica Pier uncovers the body of a girl missing for thirteen years. The body is in what looks like a huge glass ball, very much like a snow globe. Who is she, why was she killed, and what does she have to do with Roman Nevikov and the Russian Mob?
5 A Crazy Little Thing: Crews convinces Reese to have a little fun for once in her life and coaxes her out to a Rockabilly club just because they're having an Elvis impersonation night. While Crews is channeling his inner King (much to Reese's embarrassment), someone's got it in for another contestant. It's suddenly less about that crazy little thing called love, and more about that crazy little thing called murder. You can't take Crews anywhere, can you?
6 Motel California: You can come on in, but you ain't gonna leave this one, bub. Just outside of Los Angeles on a quiet and near deserted stretch of road, sits a dusty motel and a dusty poker game that ended in three dead bodies, seventeen-million in diamonds missing, and a very dead billionaire. Who connects to what? And why is Jack Reese's ring in the dead man's pocket?
7 None. What is this boring tripe?
8 Fruit. (I'll take that orange, sir.)
9 Heaven Is A Grind: Meet Lynne Gibson, a quiet barista and owner of Heaven, a small but successful cafe. She'd be ordinary if she hadn't been found buried in a pile of coffee beans in the middle of her shop. Crews and Reese must figure out what coffee has to do with murder...and what secrets are in the beans.
10 All of them! Now!
