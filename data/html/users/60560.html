Author has written 7 stories for Sailor Moon.

As you can see, I've been on this site for many years. April 2001? Good gosh, it's 2007 now! lol. I'm twenty and I'm still writing, so it was a good direction to go into. I find it most interesting to go back and reread my older stories and to see how terrible they were in how they were written. No descriptions. Bad grammar. Short chapters. The only reason I still leave them up (they are really quite embarrassing, my writing skill only showed vast improvement probably in the last two years) but I want you to see how I've grown so you can be proud of me! I had the ability back then I'd like to think, but I was impatient and only cared about submitting to get reviews. Well, I guess reviews still mean a lot to me but I call writing these fanfictions practice. You see, my dream is to be a stay at home mom who writes bestselling romance novels. But to be practical, I'm in school to be a high school English teacher but that dream will always be there. If you notice, most of my stories are just contemporary romances with the characters of Sailor Moon thrown in them. Maybe I'll be published one day, maybe.

I'm an avid romance novel reader. And I think that reading so many novels like I want to write over the years has improved my writing. Because I haven't had any extra proper teaching in writing from school over the years...I guess you just start to see what sounds good and what sounds bad. I've got an extensive collection of novels and if you want a good novel to read, just ask me, or check out my lists of good books on my Amazon.com Listmania! Some of my favorite authors are Nora Roberts, Johanna Lindsay, Lisa Kleypas, Cathy Maxwell, Julia Quinn, and Lynsay Sands. Among countless others of course. I want to be them one day. But for now, I'll settle for writing for you guys. Because if I can't please you, then how would I ever make it as a bestselling author?

I'm actually thinking about writing some help articles for you to read to help you write...I'll let you know.
