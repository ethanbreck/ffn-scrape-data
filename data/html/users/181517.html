21 September 2008

I’m not really into profile bios. Filling up the required fields are enough for me. To offer up more info, especially of a more personal nature, triggers my paranoia until I just give up and 1) run, 2) hide, 3) forget about it. I don’t make friends easily either, especially when I stopped writing. During the period of five years, I was basically inactive, coming out sporadically with one fanfic per one year and a half. The fics I came up with weren’t new either; I just tried to continue the synopses/drafts that I already had in my hard drive, hoping that I could handle everything already on my plate. Obviously I couldn’t, and I was very disappointed and hard on myself.

I’ve been depressed for years now, and writing was my only solace. It was the only thing that gave me fulfillment, the only thing that made me happy about myself, and the comments I received were proof that I wasn’t just dreaming or deluded. Yet, there came a time when writing wasn’t enough anymore. I became numb to everything. I tried caring about myself, about my responsibilities, about the people around me, even about the ozone layer, but I was apathetic, until more problems and heavier responsibilities piled up. There were also good things that happened, but it took time for me to be on a better track.

I realize that many people may have forgotten me and chalked me up for dead or retired. Many have given up on seeing the stories I had written finished.

For those of you who, for some reason, stumble upon this author page, I hope that you’ll give my stories a chance to be part of your lives as they had been to many others in the past, even for just a short while.

Thank you for reading.

21 September 2008
