The sorting hat says that I belong in Ravenclaw!

Said Ravenclaw, "We'll teach those whose intelligence is surest."

Ravenclaw students tend to be clever, witty, intelligent, and knowledgeable.
Notable residents include Cho Chang and Padma Patil (objects of Harry and Ron's affections), and Luna Lovegood (daughter of The Quibbler magazine's editor).

So, I figured an update was necessary, it's been forever since I've changed this. There isn't really much to say, I quit writing a long time ago, but haven't gotten around to taking down my stories. Maybe one day, I'll come along and rewrite them, or write something new. My account is mostly used for my favorites and my C2, A Diamond in the Rough.

My C2 contains completed Harry Potter stories. If you happen to check out this profile, and you know of some that are really good and would fit the criteria, sent me an email or something, and I'll add it!

My favorites are also completed stories that didn't fit into my C2. There are Harry Potter crossovers that are in other categories, as well as stories from Naruto, Ranma 1/2, Buffy the Vampire Slayer, Evangelion, Fullmetal Alchemist, Gundam Wing, Veronica Mars, Smallville, C.S. Lewis and more.

I have one complaint I'd like to address:

There are some things that are just not right. I have nothing against slash, if it has a good plot line and is tastefully written. But there is one thing I can't stand. Peter/Edmund is not slash, it is INCEST!! It should come with the appropriate warnings. Aside from the fact that slash in the Narnia Universe will never happen in canon (the same cannot be said for Harry Potter), nothing is more horrifying than to be reading a fic, and the High King suddenly starts smooching his baby brother. Almost every other fic in the C.S. Lewis fandom has a Susan/Peter, Lucy/Edmund, Peter/Edmund or some combination there of. The mere thought of it makes my stomach churn. So please, if you must write such stories, give them the appropriate warnings at the beginning so no one gets a nasty surprise in the middle of a otherwise decent story.
