piaffe: (pee-ahf) Verb. A rhythmic, two beat trot on the spot performed by a horse at the Grand Prix level of dressage. This movement represents years of training and preparation on the part of horse and rider and is the epitome of grace and balance. The best Grand Prix horses do not advance forward even a single inch. (Want to see it? YouTube search for Valegro or Damon Hill.) It is the grace and balance of the Grand Prix horse that I seek when I write.

Stuff I Know About: Horses, higher ed, pop culture, small town life, linguistics, working as the only girl in an all-male environment, traveling on a budget, friendships that don't make sense on paper but make life richer, and how to properly iron a shirt.

Stuff I Know Nothing About: ...pretty much everything else.
