Out, out, brief candle! Life's but a walking shadow, a poor player That struts and frets his hour upon the stage And then is heard no more. It is a tale Told by an idiot, full of sound and fury, Signifying nothing.
~ From Macbeth, by William Shakespeare.

ROS: We might as well be dead. Do you think death could possibly be a boat? GUIL: No, no, no . . . Death is . . . not. Death isn't. You take my meaning? Death is the ultimate negative. Not-being. You can't Not-Be on a boat. ROS: I've frequently not been on boats. GUIL: No, no, no--what you've been is not on boats.
 ~ From Rosencrantz and Guildenstern are Dead, by Tom Stoppard.
