06/28/2009 Sweet Jesus, I can't believe it's been almost three years since the last time I did anything here. I blame the new job I got shortly after my last update; I work an average of 45 hours a week and while I am thankful to be employed, it doesn't leave me much time or energy for writing. What little energy I have left has gone into some original fiction shorts, and I'm still debating what to do with them.

American Werewolf is, officially, abandoned. I can't seem to muster up enough interest (or energy) in Inuyasha to keep writing on it (and yes, there have been many starts and stops in the past three years before I came to this decision). I blame Resmiranda for writing Tales from the House of the Moon; I read that in the middle of writing AW and got serious performance anxiety. If anyone wants to borrow my original characters and give them some excercise, be my guest - but I would appreciate an email so I can read up on them. ;)

07/07/06 - As I'm sure many of you are aware, today is the opening day of Pirates of the Caribbean 2: Dead Man's Chest. Apparently this has revived interest in PoTC fanfiction, because I am once again recieving requests to view the cut chapter from When Walls Talk - Fire and Ice.

A little about me:

28 years old, female, happily married with two dogs. No children yet, the dogs (and husband) aren't ready to share their toys yet.

I write when and as the mood takes me. Reading is much easier.

I am a second-generation Trekkie; I grew up with an autographed picture of Brent Spiner as Data displayed with the family photographs. Yes, my therapist had a field day with that.

I thought I had left the addiction behind with my adolescence many, many years ago. And then the new Star Trek movie came out. Damn you, franchise! On the upside, I can now understand why first-generation Trekkies were nuts about Spock. Mmm. Logic.
