I guess a little background information would be nice to start off with, right? Ok. I started my love affair with anime way back in 1997/98 when I discovered Dragonball/Z on Cartoon Network. At the time, I had been in the hospital for some major surgery and I was happy to have something to look forward to each day in the three weeks I spent there. Back then, CN/Funimation were just starting to introduce anime to a mainstream audience. I really liked DB/Z, but didn't really care for anything else they had to offer. That all changed for me when Toonami first aired Gundam Wing in 2000. I loved it, and I still do to this day. After that, I was pretty happy to watch whatever shows they managed to acquire rights to.If memory serves, Yu Yu Hakusho was the next thing to gain my full attention. Somewhere around the Fall of 2005 I stopped watching anime on TV, mostly due to the fact I got tired of reruns, so I started a membership with Netflix, which allowed me watch anything I wanted without spending ungodly amounts of money. For a while, I was spending too much on anime box sets that I really only watched once and that are now in my closet gathering dust.

I am not a yaoi fan (with the exception of 3x4, and even then I will not read citrus), which means I rarely read stories in my favorite fandoms. (Just because two or more guys share the same air space doesn't automatically make them hot for each other, regardless of how gorgeous they are.)

My writing style is 'show, don't tell.'

Unfortunately, I deal with a lot of health problems, so I don't write nearly as much as I'd like.

As far as reviewing, I don't do it nearly as much as I should, but when I do, I use the "sandwich" method.

And that's pretty much it.

Thanks for visiting!

~Stormy
