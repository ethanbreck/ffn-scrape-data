What I've learned from fanfiction is...

Over the course of the years that I've been writing fanfiction, I've finally come to a realization.

I cannot write stories and receive a full and perfect result without having true inspiration.

If I just go running in with no gameplan and a silly plot bunny, it goes nowhere.

Mind you, as I'm sure my loyal fans know, (if they exist), I never really was good at plots. I'm awesome at emotion and dialogue and conveying mood and expression, but creating an actual story, well I just suck at that.

I've also learned that music is my muse. Give me a song with a narrative and I can compose an entire storyline around it. Which is why I think I should be a music video director when I grow up, but I'm going into law.

Anywho, like I was saying, I'm awesome with music. So I'm taking another crack at it. I'm going to write a lot of one shot songfics mostly, because I have a lot of ideas floating around. I've been getting a lot of inspiration lately, from other things I've been reading, and like I said, from music. So I'm going to try it again.

I used to just write stories for the sake of writing, with no plan, just to get it posted and get reviews. But I was never able to finish those stories (See: Running, Such Great Heights, and any other story tagged as incomplete, or ones I've deleted, oh and check my "My Documents" folder labeled under "Other." I have the chapter 1's written for 10 different stories, but no chapter 2's).

Like I said, not the best at plot, but I'm pretty good at continuous narratives and stream of consciousness. Even though it took me three times to spell that word right then. So I'm gonna try my hand at some one shots focusing on emotion and thoughts. Just roll with it, kay?

Anywho, if you still love me and don't hate me for leaving you hanging all this time, read my new stuff. I should have one posted by Monday at the latest.

Love,

fiery
