About me: ... I enjoy reading. That's why I'm here.

My interest in anime/manga began with Dragonball Z, just like most others in my generation (born in '86). My interest in fanfiction began with Ranma 1/2 stories. Interestingly enough, I learned everything about the series through reading fics, I never actually watched an episode or read more than a few manga chapters. Just for the sake of novelty, here are my favorite Ranma 1/2 pairings:

Ranma x Kasumi

Ranma x Nabiki

Ryouga x No One (it actually brought me joy to see him alone. Depressed Ryouga is great comedy.)

Happosai x Cologne

Ranma x Shampoo (So many obstacles to this romance, but it makes for a good story)

A few years later I started reading Naruto manga, then fanfiction, and have been enjoying it ever since.

Also: One Piece Naruto Bleach

And if Fairy Tail does well enough to be in the discussion, it'll be better than Bleach too. The best characters IMO are Inoue and Ishida, and they barely get any panels. I like IchixOri, but seriously, Ichigo's sword has more personality than he does. Never seen such a boring hero before in my life. He only has two faces: "scared for my life" and the "I'm going to win this fight to advance the plot" eye-glint. /end mini-rant

Top 5 Favorite Naruto Characters (In order) as of 9/30/11

Naruto

Itachi

Jiraiya

Hinata (would probably move up if she got more panel time...)

Shikamaru

Honorable mention: Maito Gai, Killer Bee

Favorite Naruto Canon pairings

Naruto x Hinata

That's all.

Favorite Naruto Fanfiction pairings (In order)

Naruto x Ino (I think she's just easier to write, compared to other, more distinct personalities. Typical girl, typical romance.)

Naruto x Anko (Crazy and damaged is always entertaining to watch)

Naruto x Hinata (See above.)

Naruto x Foreign Kunoichi

Naruto x Nobody (Women are troublesome)

PSA: Fics with a fem-Kyuubi pairing should label themselves NarutoxOC. Especially when a red hot fox lady meets up with 5 y/o Naruto in his mind and starts crying at him.

I'm considering writing something, but... I think I don't care enough about others' opinion. So I just daydream.

Also I'm a grad student, so I'm busy.

I never flame, (although if your story is really terrible, my criticism might hurt your feelings, but it will be a constructive attempt to help you improve,) and I always try to review if I read more than 2 or 3 chapters of a story.
