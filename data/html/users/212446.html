Modern Day Summary

In the modern era, 700 years after Lina’s death, Xellos learns of the murder of two Mazoku lords, Dynast and Dolphin by another Mazoku named Telgaln. Telgaln had used a piece of the philosopher stone and afterwards stole Dynast and Dolphin’s pieces (of which there are five in total). He, Filia and her son Val go to Egypt to look for one and find the forth piece in an as-of-yet undiscovered burial chamber, but Telgaln discovers them. He chases them to Wolf Pack Island, where they break through the magical barrier around it and find refuge with Zelas.

Zelas takes the stone for safekeeping and introduces them to the wonders of her island, and to Iyzeka, Xellos’ little sister, who has an unusual innocence. After studying Val’s unusual powers, Zelas then sends them on a mission to China to find the last piece of the stone. They pose as family members, and Filia and Xellos become closer, learning more about each other and themselves. Xellos’ emotional growth broadens, but his stability weakens. In the process, they meet new friends, and new enemies – including a new Mazoku named Kendar who works for Telgaln - and with their help they defeat Telgaln’s physical body.

However, Telgaln is quick to regain his strength and the two dragons and two demons once again flee to Wolf Pack Island, where Xellos battles Telgaln long enough for everyone to escape through the shield. He is left so damaged by the battle, however, that his physical form is destroyed, and he is brought to Zelas’ castle to heal.

While healing, Xellos ponders the discovery of one of Lina’s amulets, and begins to think dark thoughts about his existence. His newfound emotions of guilt and affection swirl within his malevolence, and he makes a crazed choice to seek out Lina’s soul in the afterlife, in the hopes that he will perhaps find an answer as to how they can defeat Telgaln, and perhaps an answer to his own struggle between evil and good.

Xellos manages to break through the barriers between this world and the next, and journeys through the Land of the Dead. He finds familiar and strange creatures there, and is confronted with himself. In the end, he returns to this plane with another of Lina’s amulets, a disturbing amnesia about his trip to the netherworld, and reawakened memories of the twisted evil of his youth.

Filia receives a vision while Xellos is away, and from it Zelas contrives a plan, to hide for 30 years on Wolf Pack Island with only 30 days passing for them. This trip into the future they are about to commit to, along with training for Filia and Valgaav. Yet Xellos still festers with newly remembered wickedness, determined to not subjugate his loved-ones to his evil. Will he be able to spare them? -- the end . . . for now.
