Favorite Heroes

Gohan Son (DBZ) - He's just awesome in early DBZ, coming up with bursts of power that buy time for someone, usually his dad, to come through and finish the bad guy off.

Goku Son (DBZ) - He's the hero, he doesn't make a mistake, and he always comes through for everyone in the end, either by beating the bad guy himself or setting things up so someone else can win, like he did with Gohan against Cell.

Yusuke Urameshi (Yu Yu Hakusho) - I just love his don't care attitude. And yet he has a hidden soft side too.

Kenshin Himura (Rurouni Kenshin) - A nice guy trying to make up for the sins of his past. Cliche, yes, but I'm a sucker for a good cliche.

Tommy Oliver (Power Rangers) - The longest serving Ranger ever. No one will ever be able to take that title from him, and only three others really come close. He also always has the coolest powers. He was lame as a Red Ranger, yet he was still able to pull off being cool.

Anko Mitarashi (Naruto): Sure, she's had fairly little screen time, but she's just awesome! A quirky, usually sadistic personality, but with a soft, tender side hidden away.

Shikamru Nara (Naruto): I just love this guy's attitude. Everything is just so 'Troublesome', and he hates fighting girls. He's practically a mysoginist, and probably would be if it wasn't too 'troublesome' to be one!

Silverbolt (Transformers Beast Wars): The Paladin of the Maximals, the embodiment of virtue. I loved the cheesy guy from his very first appearance.

Dinobot (Transformers Beast Wars): An embodiment of honor, Dinobot could very well be described as a reluctant hero.

Logan (X-Men, Wolverine): Ah, the mighty Wolverine. Considered the most dangerous mutant on the planet. With a once-mysterious past, a healing factor that can literaly bring him back from death, fighting skills developed over a century through various means, and indestructible bones with vicious claws on each hand, he is practically a perfect hand-to-hand combatant.

Kyle Rayner (Green Lantern, Ion): The Torch Bearer. At one time, the last Green Lantern, and the one who ressurected the Corps. Now the safeguard to make sure the Corps never dies, he is quite possibly the most powerful being in the DC Comics universe, his power being limited only by his imagination. And being an artist, he has a lot of imagination. He has his faults, and has suffered more personally than any other to hold the Green Lantern title (All three of his girlfriends since becoming a Lantern have been killed, though one has recently been ressurected), he is the one who discovered how to fight the yellow impurity, also known as Parallax.

Peter Parker/Spider-Man (Spider-Man comics, cartoon, movies): The web-slinging wise-cracking, smart-aleck hero of NYC, the Spectacular Spider-Man is one of my all-time favorite heroes. Having through through more personal crisis than any other hero anywhere (I dare you to find solid proof to dispute this!) and having more personal villains than anyone else I can name (seriously, the only other guy I can think of with that many villains is Batman!), Spider-Man has continuously overcome nigh-impossible odds to come out on top, a hero.
