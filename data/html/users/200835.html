Author has written 5 stories for Mummy, Misc. Plays/Musicals, Crow, Terminator, and X-Men: The Movie.

Wow. So, I used to write on fanfiction.net about fifteen years ago. Since then, I've gone off to college and outgrown many of my fandoms. However, every once in a blue moon I'll get an email notification of a new comment on one of my stories. So I've finally decided to dust off my old username and see what was on here. Here's the stats on my stories:

Medjai Night - Is finished, I'll leave it up here as a testiment to myself that I can actually finish a story (and for the off chance that anyone still reads 'The Mummy' fanfiction)

Phantom of the Opera II - You know, 'Phantom' is the one phandom (ha) I'll never outgrow. I think I might re-read this story and try to finish it up either this spring break or summer, because I vaguely remember it having potential. Also, to see if I can still write creatively!

T3: Love's Ending Before It's Begun - I'm afraid I don't have a clue where I was going with it and I'm totally through with the Terminator fandom. Plus, who needs my stories where there's another movie coming out? I think I wrote this even before T3 came out. O_O

The Crow: Wings of Revenge - Same story as T3 - don't have a clue where it was going.

The Reaction: Although my X-Men fandom has gone into slumber mode, I do still love me some Nightcrawler/Storm pairing. I may try to finish this up someday. Plus, the Wolverine movie kicked ass.

In other news, I'm a rare book dealer in Boston. I enjoy Disney movies, musicals, and a good cup of coffee/tea/mocha. My list of fandoms is ever-changing, so to list them here would be silly!

And lastly, an appology: I remember how frustrating it was to get really invested in a story, only to find out it hadn't been updated in years. I never, at the time that I wrote, thought I would be one of those authors. But school and life and growing-up took over, and fanfiction.net got all but lost in the shuffle. I hate to have upset any of my readers, but I really did just forget about my stories. I haven't written in years, and that makes me really sad. Chosing to go to school for art made me feel like it was my duty to spend any and all extra time being visually creative, and I lost the magic of writing. But I hope to get a little of that back, and I thank anyone who cares to catch up with me. :)
