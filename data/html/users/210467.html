Author has written 2 stories for Harry Potter.
As I write this, I'm missing work and not doing my homework. Actually, writing this bio is an attempt to make me forget about my guilt, shame, horror, and worry at missing my first shift of work, ever. Just kill me, why don't you. Hopefully I won't lose my job...but hey, at least I'll have a new and updated fanfiction.net bio to show for it! hm, but I guess I won't have my history project out of this, will I? Oh, forget it, I have time.

Anyway. I'm a 16-year-old Harry Potter freak with a certain love for Lily/James fics. I even have one of my own, which...(insert nervous laughter here), I haven't updated for nearly forever. It's really not my fault! I had at least two-thirds of the next chapter written, but my computer on which that chapter is saved is far, far away from me at this moment. Plus, I've lost the passion to write, temporairly anyways--writer's block if you will...and I've been rereading that blasted fic of mine and have come to the conclusion that the writing quality is absolutely horrendous. Oh well, maybe one day I'll have time for revisions, and perhaps even the next chapter.

Some pairings I enjoy unconditionally are: Harry/Ginny, James/Lily (duh), and...well, that's actually it. All other pairings rely solely on plot-writing-grammar-etc. quality. Ron/Hermione is a good one too, but only if corniess is avoided at any cost. I find that many fics with Hermione involved quickly become cheesy due to her character portrayal. Draco/anyone is okay, but you must be careful. Draco as the icy-hearted, emotionless bastard is a good characterization, but only if you know what you're doing. I like fics with obscure characters (Wood, Fred/George, Tonks, etc.), and humorous little things. I hate slash; trust me, I love gay people, but honestly--snape/harry? Let's try not to be too sick here, people. I also hate Harry/Hermione (Are you daft? Isn't it clear Ron and her belong together?) Snape/Hermione (you must ill?), Lucius/Hermione (...no comment), Zabini/Draco (Blaise is a BOY, you fools)...you get the drift, don't you?

I don't follow many fics right when they're updated, and with obsessive loyalty. However, the Slytherin Rising series by J.L. Matthews is my favorite. Also, in a completely different fic, Bring It On, by Elven Dagger. It's got quite a few cliches, but I love it to death for some reason. Crossroads by emmyjean made me cry 50 times over...well, there's about a million more. Go look in my Favorite Stories section if you long for a good fic. It's quite an assortment, ranging from incredibly cheesy and over-done (still good, let's admit) to tragically romantic, to the funniest things ever. I'm a little picky with my favorites, so rest assured that they've all passed the test.

Hope you enjoyed reading this, as I have wasted about half an hour thinking up things to write in it, a 30 minutes I could have well put towards something productive. But who says fanfiction.net isn't worthwhile?
