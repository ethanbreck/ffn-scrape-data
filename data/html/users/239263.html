Hey there! Well, let's see...this is a bio, so I suppose that you want to know allllllll about lil old me. Here goes. I'm a high school student who lives in one of the counties north of Chicago, in Illinois. I was born in Green Bay, Wisconsin, and I spend a lot of time there or in Door County. I also have some friends in the London theatre district, so I hang out there every once in a while too...and the stage manager for the theater group that I work with has a house in Michigan, so I plan on spending a lot of time during the summers up there, too! My, its hard to keep track of where the heck I am sometimes! The sad thing is I usually do that by what theater I'm in...same for time of day and day of the week. Oh, theater, you ask? Yes, I'm a techie that works in theater. I'm the sound man, man! I also do one kick-booty lighting design, if I do say so myself. I used to be an actor, but I got pulled over to the Dark Side (dark side, backstage is always dark, get it? lol, I know, that was horrible...) by some techie friends of mine and have had the best time of my life since that happened (ok, it was two years ago, but still...it has been pretty great since!). What theaters do I work in, you ask? Well, obviously, the high school that I attend has a wonderful theater department, we like to think that we are almost as popular among the student body as sports are (yeah, right, who're we kiddin')! So, I do all the shows there (2 shows and a musical in the auditorium, 2 shows and one acts in the Studio Theater). There is also a community theatre group in the next town over called CenterStage, which puts on two winter shows, a fall show, a summer musical, and sometimes a spring Shakespeare performance every year. So, along with classes, that keeps me busy. I have met so many wonderful people in theatre, it is unbeleivable! All the crew is pretty much the same locally, so we are like one big family, and do everything together (its pretty hard to keep up a relationship with someone outisde of theatre, what with our schedule, anyways), and it is great. This summer particularly, I have been hanging out with one young college student who I will call Ben here, and who I have fallen madly in love with (in the friendship way, you twits! lol!). I have practically been living with him and his ex-girlfriend(Whitney) for the whole run of my last musical, which I just finished at the end of July, and it was so much fun! The two of them are so funny. You'd think that they were cousins or something, the way they act. They broke up when he left for college, but still are inlove with each other in a way, though neither one would admit it to the other. They are now the best of friends, and get along perfectly, and for some strange reason have decided to latch onto me and adopt me in a wierd mentor-ish way. It makes me feel very happy to think that two people as wonderful as those two care enough to spend time with me. Anyway, he goes to U of I, and thats where I want to go, too, so I am now starting to hang out in Champagne-Urbana and have already started getting hooked up with that crowd, sort of. I guess I'll be hanging around Kranert alot in the future (lol)! Anyway, thats enough about theatre, I guess, although that basically constitutes what I do. The two people that I am talking about above are in Michigan right now, so I am seriously lonely at the moment, and feeling kind of depressed bc all of the people who I was seeing on a daily basis are gone, until I see them back at school, and in some cases, maybe forever. There are a couple people, (Steve and Kelly Klein) who are absolutely AWESOME people, even though they are actors which I don't hold against them, who I may not see again. They come from Glenview, went to Stevenson High School, and are now at college. Steve goes to a performing arts school in New York, he's starting his sophmore year there (same age as Ben), and is one of the most talented actors I have worked with, apart from pros, and has one of the nicest personalities that I have encountered in a human yet. Kelly is going to U of I (I think/hope!), and is starting her freshmen year there, and is another rising star, and a real sweetheart. Unless they come back to CenterStage next summer, I will never see them again (sniff!). I hope that they do, it's people like them who have beautiful voices that make the sound guy(me) look good. Anyway, I hope that Ben and Whitney get back soon, because when they do we are going out for steak, as kind of a last hurrah before Ben goes back to college, and our little group gets split up. Luckily, Whitney will be a senior this coming year, and I will be a sophmore, so we will have another year together before we have to say goodbye (maybe for good). We are already planning to live in the Raymond Moore Auditorium for the fall show (currently undecided, but it'll be a straight-up play, no music or dance. I hope that we do The Crucible, altough that would be a better Studio Theater play). It is really a bittersweet thing for a run of a show to end. The people who you have grown so attached to in the last few months are all drifting away, back to "normal" life. For you, it would be normal day-to-day life. For us theatre people, it is that horrible drifting time between shows, when you have to recharge your batteries, you don't see much of the people that you have grown to love, and there is an emptiness in our lives that screams to be filled with another show, especially when we find ourselves sitting at home at night writing stuff and watching TV, and occaisonally calling each other on the phone to check up on things before we start seeing each other again. The end of a summer run right before school starts is especially hard. You will see most of these people again in maybe a month, when you go back to school, which is long enough to make you miss them horribly, and short enough that you cannot bring yourself to justify making special trips just to see them. Their families also want to take vacations, and a month is a perfect buffer zone to do it in. So, we are all thrown apart to different corners of the globe, so that we miss each other horribly, and the long distance bills are far too high to keep in tough with one another, and we all long for school to start for the sole reason that we will see our friends (who am I kidding, our second familes) again. I am experiencing that between-show depression right now, and cannot get to sleep because of it, although I am exhausted by two and a half months of 15 hour or longer days. And so, dear reader, I leave you now to try and sleep once more, consoling myself with the thought that yes, I will see Ben and Whitney in about a week, I have whole 'nother year with Whitney, and Ben will never really have left us, becuase we love him too much. This started out as a bio, and really turned into a short synopsis of life as I know it right now. Hope you liked it, and I promise to make it it's own peice in a little bit.

I'll write again soon, I promise it.
-Sean
