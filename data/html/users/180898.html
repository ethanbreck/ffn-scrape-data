Current Work:

Star Trek: Surprise - Being a huge nerd, I love Star Trek. Unsatisfied with the fan fiction for Trek on this site and all over the internet (although my long searching had produced a few gems), I decided six or seven years ago to write the ultimate new Trek. My first attempt failed... miserably. It suffered from all of the problems of other Trek fan fiction. For example, the characters were too perfect, or too deliberately un-perfect. That was just the tip of the iceburg when it comes to the problems.

So I regrouped and revised. I came out with something completely different. I wrote nearly 65 thousand words and had meticulously editted and revised the first 35 thousand. I let it sit for some time, almost a year, and then re-read it. Pure drivel. I scrapped the idea and started from scratch.

I came up with a new concept: instead of being a pure Star Trek exploration series, I wanted it to be more in the genre of political/spy thriller. I was partly inspired by Articles of the Federation and I will use that as source material for any concepts that have to do with the Federation Council and President. For example, Nan Bacco is President of the UFP. Other than that, I will diverge from the Pocket Books timeline and I will likely throw the events of Star Trek: Destiny out completely. Meant as a serial, character-driven set of stories, we will see our crew grow and mature and not always for the better.

The main driver of conflict will be a secret struggle over the very soul of the Federation. Captain Pavlik of the USS Surprise, our protagonist, has run afowl the designs of the dreaded and secretive Section 31. He has turned to his closest friends - the only people he can trust - for help. Not just to prevent the Section from destroying his career, but to put an end to their organization once and for all. To do so, Pavlik must tread carefully in what is supposed to be the safest place in the Federation: a Starfleet vessel. He has to decide who among his crew he can trust - such as the raw and tactless Chief of Security April Petrovic or his young protégé Kristen Ferris, Chief of Operations - and any misjudgement of character could lead to disaster. He also has to deal with an ambitious counsellor named der Vries, whose intentions - and loyalties - remain opaque.

And the Section is not the only thing Captain Pavlik has to worry about. The defeat of Cardassia and the fall of the Romulan Empire has resulted in an end to the raison d'être of the Federation-Klingon Alliance, allowing voices of dissent in either realm to gain traction in public discourse. Starfleet believes that Federation idealism and Klingon conservatism are incompatible without a common goal like maintaining the balance of power in the Alpha and Beta Quadrants and now that the Federation-Klingon alliance stands alone as the region's only major power that tension - even war - is impossible to avoid.

Book One: A Private Little War, has been completed. Corrections and suggestions are welcome.

Book Two: Sparrows on the Rooftops (working title) is being written and is about 40-50% finished. In this Book, Section 31 and Federation-Klingon relations are on the backburner, and the crew of the Surprise must deal with the everyday problems of people living on the frontier - scarcity, cultural misunderstandings and a tradition of defying authority started by the extinct, but not forgotten, Maquis.

About the Author: I enjoy writing as a hobby, and have written fan fiction since I was a teenager and posted them here (since taken down.) I like to delve into the aspects of a made-up universe that aren't well explained.
