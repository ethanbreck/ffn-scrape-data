I'm 32 years old and an avid Harry Potter fan.

My favourite book from the Harry Potter series: Prisoner of Azkaban

My favourite characters: Sirius Black, Remus Lupin, Harry Potter, Severus Snape and Albus Dumbledore... I didn't really know what to make of Dumbledore in the first 4 books. But now, after OotP, he appears just as fallible as the others. He isn't omniscient, he makes mistakes but I truly believe that he wanted (and still wants) to protect Harry from every harm his connection with Voldemort could cause him. And that he finally showed some of the affection (and grief) he must be feeling for Harry simply touched me. And Snape... well, I guess not everything is as it appears after HbP.

I think that DH managed to bring the series to an ultimately satisfying conclusion but, frankly, to me the Potter-series lost some of its magic after book 4. Those first 4 parts will always have a special place in my heart, they set up such a high standard that the later 3 parts just failed to uphold.

Nonetheless, I'm still very much into fanfiction - perhaps because I see some of that promised potential better fulfilled in these works than in anything JKR managed to put forth. And honestly, I think JKR read too much fanfiction herself at times because some of her ideas awfully reminded me of what I've read quite a bit earlier here on this site...

So, since now every fanfic is categorically an AU, what stories do I prefer to read?

I LOVE stories where Sirius has to show his parental side towards Harry. Unlike in some stories I've read so far, I really think that Sirius escaped from Azkaban to care for Harry, not out of duty to James. If that were the case, then why risk detection while hoping to get a glimpse of Harry in Magnolia Crescent?

I also like Snape/Harry-mentor-stories very much. I think that Snape and Harry have much in common and once they overcome their mutual dislike they could be able to form a working relationship. Harry needs a firm hand to guide him - especially since he's so unwilling to place his trust in an adult figure and thinks he has to take on everything by himself - and I strongly believe that Snape needs to be pulled out of the hole he's been digging for himself his whole life.

In essence, stories have to contain:

lots of character-interaction
emotions - they make the characters and the stories alive

What I absolutely don't like to read:

spelling mistakes - sorry, but it's really a major annoyance if I have to figure out the words before being able to actually read the story
misspelling of names, places, characters - it's called fanfiction, and it's not farfetched to think that a fan might actually be able to spell the names, and places of his fandom correctly
