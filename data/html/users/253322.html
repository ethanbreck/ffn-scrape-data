Notes on Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (spoilers)

okay, admittedly, i got the book at 9pm and read until 9am-i'm stupid, so what.

I just have to say two things and make them public, thenall can hate me as they wish:

:inhales: I still think that Snape may not be a bad guy :jugglesuncertainly: yes, that is on a string of hope, make no mistake about it (like...murder...of HIM...yeah, that was doing some major evil) but...i dunno, it just seems too...i don't know. But I still think Snape could benot trulyevil.He completely guilt tripped for Narcissa in the begininning,it was their past that touched him. It was dark, but it made sense. If Snape still wanted to help the light side, he'd have to kill Dumbledore, wouldn't he? Or else he'd just...die. So maybe the vow was made at a point of weakness. um...everyone makes mistakes? HELL, even I felt kind of sorry for the Malfoys by the end of it, evenafter I was completely outraged about whatMalfoy did to Harry on the train. ButHarry felt pity forMalfoy, too, at the end...I told you, i'm stupid.

Secondly, all right, is it just me or does Harry/Harry's scar seem to be a Horcrux? I mean, (1) If Nagini is a Horcrux after all, wouldn't that explain why he dreamt from the snake's point of view in OoTP? He was technically connected to Nagini with...a piece ofVoldemort's soul...? (This is all off the top of my head by the way. I don't read the books muchmore than once, thoughI think I will this one)He asked Snape why he dreamed that wayand got yelled at for saying You-Know-Who's name. (2) Voldemort did put "some of himself" into Harry when he gave him that scar...It could happen. Uhh...right, third point, do i have a third point?...uhhh...Well, (3:sorta) Isn't Harry sort of Gryffindor? Voldemort wanted something of Ravenclaw or Gryffindor...

But I know! Why would Voldemort purposely do that? There's the argument it happened accidently, possibly following along the lines of the power of love that Voldemort knew not of. Thus far those side effects for him have been squeaky for him. And then there's confusion that if Voldemort does kill Harry then technically is he making himself weaker? What actually happens when a Horcrux(am i even spelling that right?) is broken? Presumably, since destroying them will make him mortal, you'd think that his soul is restored to him. But when one assumes they make an 'ass' out of 'u' and 'me'...(blame my AT English teacher for that, she's fond of the anecdote). But it makes more sense thatby destroying the Horcruxes actually means destroying the soul completely, doesn't it?That would have the same effect, would it not? With no piece of him left on earth, he would die just the same. So which? Again I ask, what would killing Harry do if that were true? There's the whole 'neither can live while the other survives' bit...but wait a minute! If he did die and Harry survives then he'd still be technically alive in Harry or his scar. The prophecy would have to say neither of them live at all! Okay, I withdraw my entire arguement but for the sake of not wasting the time it has taken to type it up, I'm going to leave it here. I told you, i'm stupid.

Whatever! There's definitely more and I have to wait for this anxiousness to die down before I can wait-I'm guessing it to be about two years- before the next book comes out. :gulps:
