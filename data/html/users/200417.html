Hello anyone who is reading this, I may be flamed but something has been on my mind for a while.

I don't know why everyone is so overly critical on this site. I have seen so many downright mean comments that I thought could have been phrased much better. Many people join this site in order to become better writers. You can't become a better writer without practice. I think we should encourage people to write what they enjoy writing and give constructive criticism if they ask for it. If they are proud of what they have accomplished then we should be happy for them, provide them with ideas or suggestions but not with flames. I used to really enjoy writing but now I am rather worried about whether people will flame or “harshly criticize”. I speak from experience, if you get a good review you are more likely to try harder in the next chapter. If you get a bad one, you are more likely to stop altogether and I don’t think that that is the idea of this site at all. I enjoy reading stories, and I enjoy encouraging new writers (I also consider myself a new writer and enjoy being encouraged). If someone new is just trying to improve their writing and they get flamed on something they were proud of they will most likely decide that if people are going to be so mean then they don’t want to write anymore. I don’t know what makes some people feel they have duty to flame the living daylights out of any and all stories not up to their standards, but I hope they don’t enjoy being mean because that would just make me lose even more faith in this species and I already have so little. I would also like to add, on the topic of Mary-sues; Romance novels are a VERY successful form of literature and the Mary-sue writer today may be the nationally acclaimed Romance Novelist of tomorrow. There, now that I got that rant off my chest, I feel better. If I get flamed for this I am not going to respond for I feel that there are plenty of flame wars out there already. I am also sorry if anyone feels singled out, if you do, well, it isn’t you, it’s everyone.
