REVIEW TO Order Of The Phoenix.*

WHAT on earth has J.K Rowling done to all the characters we have come to know and love? Every character that has gone through some sort of change hardly seems recognizable, and for those who's lives are pretty much the same, Rowling still seems to develeloped a different sort of personality for them.
I mean, yeah, it was bound to happen; puberty during a war will do that to you, but Rowling begins the story with what seems to fable a character to be real. It's been too damn long (from the Goblet of Fire) for Rowling to start Harry off with his MAJOR attitude change, it needed to be a gradual thing for it to really have any effect on the reader. Quarter way through the book you start to think your faced with an abnoxious little twat, who's too big for his own boots, than a hero that has the destiny of the entire wizarding world in his lap. There are most definately high's about the book however; all the secrets and surprises, not to mention sudden deaths, have quite an impact on the reader, but what the book seems to be lacking, which I personally think kept the four former books so glued together and respected by audiences; was that people could relate too, or at least have an understanding of, what Harry was going through. In the fifth book, it seems as though Harry is too busy yelling at everyone, accusing characters of things that were as much as his fault as it was his, than portraying that realistic and down-to-earth charm people everywhere came to love.
I mean, yeah, it is a book about a wizarding world, where mirror's talk, people change into animals and giants sleep in forbidden forests, but the underlying story, is what I think is lacking. For it's length the story seems to unravel to quickly for readers to get the slightest chance to understand Harry's problems, and then when that chance arrives, you get completely disgusted by him for what he is thinking about towards loyal friends. *cough* WOES OF MRS. WEASLEY chapter *cough*
All in All, being a Harry Potter book, of course it is brilliant, but in my standards, not at all what it could have been. And no, I'm not referring to all the rumours that were posted on the net, I pretty much ignored them, and fanfiction, of course this is all just a fantasy (as much as the books them-selves), but Rowling could have done so much better, even if it may have taken longer to write and produce; at least it would have been worth the wait to read.
