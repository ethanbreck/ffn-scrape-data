Proud Supporter of the preservation of True Fandoms everywhere

What does that mean? Well, it means that I don't change the characters too much in the fanfiction that I write - ESPECIALLY ones where those resposible for the creation of said fandom have parted ways. Those fandoms are especially important to preserve, since all that will remain, after all is said and done, is the memory - the fans should be fan enough to uphold the characters the way they were created. Common violations are:

1) Characters that are used in yaoi/yuri/shounen-ai/shojou-ai/slash (all fancy ways of saying same-gender relationships) when the characters WERE NOT CREATED that way.
2) Characters that fall in love that would not have, by any means, come into a relationship any other way, save by the fanfiction author's hand.
3) Characters that are absurdly OOC for no reason at all (eg. a character who normally hates the world should stay that way UNLESS something happens in the fic to change that)
4) Mary Sues

Any drastic changes that occur to characters (such as those both mentioned and not mentioned above) are rectafied (changed back to the way they were before) by the end of the fic or series.

If you want to support too, add that little blurb posted above to your bio; and help keep fandoms they way they were created by their authors. You're an author too - put yourself in their shoes. Or e-mail me. If enough people support this, I'll make a C2 community.
