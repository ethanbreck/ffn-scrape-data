Hello everybody :)

The following message has been pasted to my old message board and my former web site.

If you would like to read this message at my old site Go Here
Or click the 'Homepage' link above. This message was meant for my old 'Shin Shrine' site. It makes more sense read from there. When I say, 'this old place,' I'm speaking of my former site, just to let you know. I pasted it here for those who land here and get confused about where everything went. Your many e-mails gave me this idea, so maybe now you'll all understand what happened. :)

About my old site: I decided to put this up for the last time on this old place because of a few reasons. People keep asking me about this place and wondering why I used to have Lady Shin as a pen name. Questions about why I dumped this site and why the other one is under my husband’s management with his affiliates from his other site (they’ve been joined)

It’s simple: I left this place and gave my management up to my husband because of my writing that’s blossomed again. This is to put these many tiring questions to rest; I’ve been receiving through the mail and the message board on the other site. Since there is a link on the merged site, I thought that when my fans are redirected here they will learn the whole story without me constantly explaining through the mail. I’m very busy (accent on ‘busy’) and I haven’t the time for answering all of those letters. Thank you for wondering about it, though. I do appreciate your concerns and many questions.

So, now that you’re here the following should explain things. Instead of the confusion this would cause on the other site, it’s better to put this on the site I’ve been questioned about.

By the way:

Hi everyone. *Smiles*

I sure hope you are all well and holding up through this glorious season. As all of you know I have my book for sale at B&N and the ISBN# is very searchable at this time. So YES! You can order it through your locale bookseller anywhere on the globe. Just go to my literary site or your local bookstore. The ISBN# is on my other site as you may or may not know by now.

I've been busy working on the next book in this series and I've combined some sought after poetry I've written in this new one. I can't wait for you all to read my forthcoming novel. These sets of poems follow the plots of each book. Most are in Shakespearian sonnet form. I have a few that follow the tetrameter form as well. As you, all know by now with my first completed book of poetry written in many different classic forms I’m quite fond of this type of pure beautiful literature. A few non-traditional free verse pieces I’ve incorporated into this book series is written in iambic pentameter, (five metric feet per line) however, it remains free verse in this case. Maybe some might call them songs, like the Tolkien songs in The Lord of the Rings series.(That’s what my agent says) You know the one’s that run through all six books of the LotR series. I’m always amazed every time I read them. I just purchased the 50th anniversary print and it’s gorgeous. Anyone who owns it should know, and those who don’t you won’t be disappointed.

Hey, did you know a little known fact? I’ll bet you didn’t. My grandfather went to Oxford University at the same time Tolkien was there, as a professor of Anglo-Saxon, not to mention Tolkien’s other grand academic titles, and my grandfather knew him. They went to the local pub together many times. The attraction apparently started when my grandfather told him of his interest in the literary arts, and they shared many other interests out side of school. My father attended Cambridge like my mother so they weren’t Rodes like him.

I can't say how proud I am to have my work sold again in brick-and-mortar stores across the globe. You have no idea how it feels. I did do a WotC book from the Gathering series, but that was way before my mother died, and in those days, I had various magazine stories and science fiction published in the Dr. Who classic comics in December 1994, (my first publication in my teens!) sadly they’re closed now. I’ve also written many times (A total of six stories) for Dr. Who magazine, still in operation. An accomplishment I’m very proud of.

Yes, I’ve done seven book signings on this new venture at several B&N UK and Borders over the past year. I’ve done publicity shots (for promotional purposes) and radio interviews. The lot. I have left the childish world of anime because I have no time to do anything with it, nor do I want to. My children are a bit tired of it too. I guess it works for an adolescent mind frame and that’s why so many youngsters like it. My excuse is, I was drawn into it by my children and it seemed a bit interesting, however, how many times can they blow Goku up and make it something you want to actually see on numerous occasions? The same thing goes for the rest of anime, in my opinion, they are so predictable and the acting is awful J version and USA.

Not to mention the horrid sound they make when powering up and fighting. They sound more like constipation than anything else.

Well, I watched part of a DVD on the Buu saga the other night (before Christmas day and Boxing Day over here) and it felt like I took a spoonful of nasty medicine. How bad could it have been? The acting is terrible and Shin’s voiceover in both countries is bad. Even my husband agrees. The acting on the US version is worse. He really does sound like a woman; Kent doesn’t do the character justice just as they told me at that convention in N.Y a few years ago, when I brought DBZ up. I always knew that too. The J version sounds a bit better but they are in the same category in the believability range and that’s nil.

They sound so ridiculous to me now, even in the scene when Buu attacks Kaioshin, I laughed because of the ridiculous noises he made while beating him. Sorry, to anyone who still likes him but isn’t it about time you all grew up? Unless of course you’re a child, then it’s understandable. I know older people like him, I did, but I got over it fast. After I wrote that dare story about ‘power crystals’ and ‘Sallaya’, I didn’t really like the character any more. I only kept the site for my kids and when they grew tired of the whole thing, I dumped it giving it over to my husband for that reason and the aforementioned time issue, for my contract and new literary site that you all know by now and have flocked to. I thank you all for that.

The dare story about the Crystals happened because of, Cindy. She knew I’d left writing for a while do to my mother’s premature death that year. It totally threw me for a loop and I didn’t care about my writing and mostly left things rough, unedited, and first draft quality. She wanted to stimulate my senses and get my creative juices flowing. (Pardon the cliché) So she threw this silly idea at me. Well, as you all know I did it, but my plots were awful and I didn’t care. We were talking about the magazine I was published in and the Wizards of the Coast book I wrote a while before this happened. She said that these stories in anime are from writers and some write Fan fiction. Well, you can imagine my disgust of realising people wrote romantically about a cartoon character. I cringed at the thought since I’m a serious writer I really had no idea so many stories would be romantic about a drawing. I guess the world of teenage fantasy is broad and very queer. Another reason I really didn’t enjoy writing it.

Well, to make a long story short she challenged me to write a Mary Sue story about Kaioshin. ‘Are you kidding!’ I screamed in horror. I mean the thought of a pointy-eared lavender oddball like that in a romantic scene made me shudder. I still wasn’t over my mother. I mean she had just passed away a week before all of this challenge.

So, yes, it became, rushed, raw, and bloody awful (Pardon my mix of US and UK I'm a Brit that lived in the US for a time.) I didn’t care what my writing really looked like, as said before. That wasn’t the point back then. It certainly wasn’t something I felt proud of and nothing I would have ever shown to a copy editor. Fan Fiction stories of fluff and cartoons are taken rather lightly at my publisher. My literary agent told me that if all I ever wrote were FF then he would have never been interested in my work.

You have to write original works to be published and I always knew that because of the writing I’d done before I found Fanfiction.net. That place was an outlet for my silly writing and the fact I dedicated a book to Kent meant nothing to me, as far as being serious. Good grief, I did that for him because he was so nice and asked me about my writing and really wanted to see it. I decided to pay him back for visiting my site, and honoring my children’s request, signing my guest book, and giving me his voice clip not to mention being a great support over petty nonsense caused by foppish witted children. So yes, I did dedicate a book to him for fun. It was only FF and not an original mind you.Although, some slipped off their cracker over it and made fools of themselves. That was quite funny, by the way. *Grins*

Anyway, if you’re here out of curiosity, brought over by my other site that’s the short version of why I made the other one. I’m too busy with my contract (it’s for five years) and I have a very busy writing schedule. Just know I’m very happy and doing incredibly well. I’m where I want to be here in Belgravia. Alan says hi too. E-mail me for more information on things. If you’re a true friend you’ll have it, believe me.

Oh, and to make it clear once again. The book cannot be published on my site anywhere on the net. It has to do with first publishing rights. Anything that’s been published through the net or elsewhere to be made public, diaries included, are considered a public domain, unless password protected. If you’re trying to become published, and I wish you much luck, NEVER, post any of your work to the public. You will lose first publishing rights and of course your contract, or potential contract at once. This also includes any plot situations concerning FF in some cases.I also changed my pen name when I became a professional writer, so no, I'm not listed as Lady Shin.

I chose that name because of my real family title (Lady) The name 'Shin' was used with it because of the site. I would never want to be acknowledged in my circles or any public circles as 'Lady Shin' regarding anime. It would be horrid. That's making a mockery out of my real title of 'Lady'.

You can also see my interview regarding my book release last July, and I talk some about my forthcoming novel in the series. It has a direct link to the NYT.

Bye everyone, stay happy and healthy and have a great life to you all.
