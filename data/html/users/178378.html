Author has written 13 stories for Inuyasha, Final Fantasy VIII, Secret/Legend of Mana, Kaze no Stigma/風のスティグマ, Birdy the Mighty, 3x3 Eyes, and Teen Titans.

Update: I'm aware of the situation with missing chapters from When Fire Fell. I'm not going to fix it until I do my next chapter. I know it's confusing and all and I'm sorry about that! When I do post the next chapter, bear in mind you'll see ALL of my chapters vanish for a few hours then reappear as I re-upload every single chapter from the beginning to the most current chapter. So when you get a mess of chapter notifications, just sit back and let them load for a few hours. When you get around to chapter 18-19ish you'll know that's the start of the new one.

Update 2: I have Tumblr! It seems to be mostly for Teen Titans drabbles but hey it's there. Greenll3unny. There are cleverer people on there than me so go check it out for them.

I also just noticed that I've been writing fan fiction for over ten years. Wow, that's more than a DECADE of bad writing! LOL

I am Jo, hear me roar. Well, I'm 28, with a BA in Studio Art. I've been posting fan fiction since I was sixteen but I actually started writing it as early as eighth grade. Currently, I have several stories in the works with several more plot bunnies ready and waiting to be posted so keep an eye out for more things in the future. I hope you guys enjoy my stories as much as I enjoy writing them!

If you want me to get stories out faster I really encourage everyone to look me up... message, review, or e-mail. I'm more than happy to talk about my ideas of where I want the story to go. It's a huge help to me to get lots of input. I feel that authors sometimes cling too tightly to what they want in a story and refuse to change poor plot choices. I think that the best writers are open to input and open to change. I'm not saying that everyone should bend stories to the will of the people, even I won't do that all the time, but at the same time, being entirely inflexible can be devastating to a writer.

Yes, most of the anime that I write about is the older stuff from way back when. There's a reason for that. Some of it is that I grew up with those shows as a teen and I'm very passionate about them. But, there's another reason, too. Older shows tend to have endings that didn't have good relationship resolutions OR they had really, really crappy endings. I can't stand that! That's what got me into fan fiction to begin with.

Anyway, that's all I have to say about that.

P.S. Yes, that is me cosplaying as Belldandy in my profile picture.
