How do I sum up in a couple paragraphs what has taken 21 years to craft? snerk I'm sorry, that giggling you hear was my alter-ego, PPC Agent Ash Starr. Ignore her. So, a little about Where I'm Coming From:

I'm a senior in my final semester at Arizona State, graduating in May. happy dance

Writer. Equestrian. Actress. Idealist. Hopeless romantic. Daydreamer. Sci-fi geek. Fangirl. I have a whole slew of fandoms, and not enough time to adequately cover all of them, or wax eloquent on my opinions of them. A few words about the major ones, though:

Babylon 5: Best. TV. Show. Ever. Probably my #1 fandom, although you'll never catch me writing fanfic about it. JMS is sacred.
Lord of the Rings: The books. All hail Tolkien. I appreciate the movies from a visual aspect (coughfangirlcough), but I think PJ really screwed up. Another one I probably won't write fanfic for, because it's a continuum that really doesn't need any more Mary Sues.
Star Wars: The one that I will pontificate one. SW is near and dear to my heart, mostly because it was my first fandom that hit "major obsession" level. Self-proclaimed Skywalker Stalker. Luke, that is. One of the first fictional characters I think I fell in love with. The prequel trilogy makes me want to beat my head against a wall. The whole direction of the EU makes me want to hurl. I'm gonna get strung up on a force-pike for this one, but here goes: I absolutely adore the character of Callista, and think she and Luke are perfect for each other. Barbara Hambly's writing is first class, all the way. As far as I'm concerned, anything after Planet of Twilight is null and void. All the EU-thor and Mara fans can go ahead and cut my down with lightsabers now, I've said my peace. That being said, this is what I would consider my "fanfic 'verse", just because I can't screw it up any more than it already is. /soapbox!rant.

Some of my other fandom interests include Discworld, Stargate SG-1, Alias, and Firefly. Fav. movies to include: The Wizard of Oz, Ever After, Monty Python and Disney's Robin Hood. Lots and lots of books - I'm an avid reader. Anything by Barbara Hambly is a guaranteed win with me. Especially her Star Wars novels. Despite all this, most of my writing gets done as original fiction, but I like to dabble in fanfic.
