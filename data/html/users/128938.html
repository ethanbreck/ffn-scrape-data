Author has written 11 stories for Gundam Wing/AC, Harry Potter, and One Piece.

I've been at this now for over ten years.

Ten years.

TEN YEARS. (actually, its been about 14 now)

I've never quite been able to kick the fanfic addiction, and while sometimes I feel a little shamefaced towards something that others have moved beyond as they aged -- considering it and myself both childish -- I'm really never going to go against what makes me happy. I'm eternally boy-crazy, that's what I am, whether or not anyone else disapproves isn't going to be my problem.

I like to think I'm a good writer, but not a lot of what I'll have in here will necessarily be good since there is a lot I'll be re-posting for the sake of posterity. In 2001, there wasn't a whole lot along the lines of quality when it came to m/m fanfiction and though I managed not to completely garble the English language into incomprehensible suck, my writing was incredibly mediocre back then. I wrote a lot of RPS following that, which was much improved, but I'm not inclined to go that direction anymore. These days, though I still can't tell a proper story for the life of me and only really excel at descriptive writing, I'm probably good at what I do. If I still couldn't write after a 14 year stretch of being consumed by nothing but that (though, admittedly, eight of those was dedicated solely to original fiction), that'd be awfully depressing to think.

That said, I don't always like to toot my own horn -- its only fun if I've got the right sheet music. :D

More often than not, I stress and worry and panic as I write, and want to print my pages out so I can have something physical to rip to shreds and light on fire. :X

I tend to have frequent internet-identity-crisis (as a friend once coined it), and change my author name often. I'm constantly undergoing weird self-evolutions and it seems like these come and go like fashion lines -- spring and summer, to fall and winter -- and I really dislike being identified by one title... or fandom. I've never felt like anything defined me just right. Which is silly; its just a pen-name, right? But I hate being called by them, and hate hearing my real name online too, since anonymity is particularly important. Still, my old work can largely be found under alt06 and scarletmeans. Who and what I'll be tomorrow, exactly... is anyone's guess.
