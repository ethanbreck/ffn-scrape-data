Life and Writing

I pull inspiration for my writing from every aspect of my life, and I always have a story or a scene on my mind to write. It’s just the actual writing part that becomes troublesome. It’s easy to see a picture in my head and know exactly how I want it to be, it’s a lot harder to actually write it out that way. Secondly, I do not plan my stories the way other writers do. I make no lists or summaries regarding chapters. At most, I have an idea of where I want to go with a story, but I don’t know how I’m going to get there until I write it. Outlines do not exist in my world, because they usually just go to pot anyways. I ignore them, I forget about them, I don’t use them. They’re useless to me. I write as it comes, and as I can take the time.

In this way, inspiration does not always come when I need it to. Writer’s block is my most loving enemy, and it likes to accost me whenever possible. At these times, I try my best to struggle through, and I make it eventually. My best ideas come when I’m just shoving words from my brain, through my nerves, and out my fingertips to the keyboard. To be blunt: I blurt out whatever comes to mind, and it usually works. If it doesn’t, I try again, all the way until I have a sentence, then a paragraph, then a chapter, and eventually a storyline.

For these reasons, stories do not always come in the timeliest fashions. But be patient, it’s there. If I changed the way I wrote, it wouldn’t be my writing.
