Oh, and my Titans stories have a few obscure references in them, I'll list the ones I remember here as I publish them.

In Terra Morte,

Beast Boy listens to a song called "Save Me" by a group called Damageplan. I put it in because I wrote that part shortly after Darrell Abbot was killed

Slade's appearance, sans mask, is based on his look in the comic books, white hair, goatee and a white eye patch.

In God Machine,

Several dead hero's are mentioned. Don Hall, who is the original Dove, and Barry Allen, the second Flash, both killed in the Crisis on Infinite Earths story arc, and Oliver Queen who is the Green Arrow (and yes, alive again)

In Of Love and Death

Starfire and Robin call each other by their real names. Starfire's is Koriand'r, or Kori for short, and Robin is Dick Grayson (and please, yes, he is Dick Grayson. In the show, he becomes Nightwing in the future, and in the episode 'The Haunted', Raven goes into his head and sees two people falling from a trapeze. That is how Dick Grayson's parents died, and has nothing to do with anyone else who was ever Robin. So there :p)

Some time ago, Speedy became Arsenal, and changed his image, relying on a lot of different weapons instead of just arrows. I just put this in...well cause I wanted to.

In the epilogue, the bio's of four people, Kid Flash, Superboy, Wonder Girl, and Speedy (Mia Deardon, the new Speedy) are shown. If you combine these people with the Titans in the cartoon, you get the current comic book line up of the Teen Titans (Sorry, but I am a huge comic book fan)

In the second epilogue, Coast City is mentioned. If you aren't a fan of comic books, this might require some explanation. Coast City was wiped completely off the map by an alien named Mongul, around the time Superman was killed in 1994. As a result, about 7 million people were killed, and the whole area was irradiated. If you figure this into what the Flash said in my story, it really was a sick joke. I thought it was funny though :)
