"There is a technical term for those who mistake the opinions and beliefs of characters in a novel for those of the author.The term is 'idiot.'"
-- S.M. Sterling

This account is no longer actively used to post anything. Once upon a time, yes, but since then a wave of rather draconian policies have swept in over the Pit of Voles. I didn't like, but I understood, the removal of fics warranting an NC-17 rating. It was the removal of any and all fiction that even so much as quoted a single line of anything, be it from a poem, song, or the original body of work, for any purpose whatsoever that made me walk away and never look back.

The increasing wonkiness of formatting also bothered me. While WYSIWYG is nice and quick and easy, I'll admit, when it comes to actual posting and story content, it's screwed up so much that I'm a hardcore raw coder. So I post my fics elsewhere.

You can find my works on InsaneJournal at The Chaotic Library under the pen name Kuchenhexe posted through my writing account.

The plotbunny warren is empty, and my muse is in the Bahamas last I heard. But when it's in town, I write for Fullmetal Alchemist primarily these days, but not exclusively. I still hold a lot of love for Slayers, and would like to write in that fandom again.

I keep a somewhat more up-to-date list of my fandoms and where my interest lies (and when I'm ambitious someday, I'll include a listing of WIPs here) on my writing journal, which I use to post to Chaotic Library, or post locked snippets of WIPs and notes for my work.

Profile: Plotbunny Attack at InsaneJournal

I might use this account in the future to post teasers and lead-ins for finished fics along with a link leading to the full thing, but I dunno yet. As it is, I only remember this account is here when I get an email notifying me of this or that. :D

I do intend to try to finish my Illusion of Truth trilogy. I'd hate to go and not get it completed. I've never quite given up on it. I've been forced into early retirement due to being diagnosed with Stage IV Breast Cancer, otherwise known as metastatic breast cancer. There's no cure for that. I probably won't remember to update this whenever it is that I finally do have go on hospice, so I'm linking to my blog, which will get updated. Feel free to PM me with questions. Metastatic Breast Cancer is different from "regular" Breast Cancer, and it's become a personal crusade of mine to spread awareness. There's no question I'll consider too personal.

Metathriving at Blogspot
