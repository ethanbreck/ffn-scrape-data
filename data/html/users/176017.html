Author has written 16 stories for Super Smash Brothers, Secret/Legend of Mana, Final Fantasy XIII, Lufia, Star Ocean, and Secret of Evermore.

New year new me! You can find me on fiction sites under the name Claude the Author from now on. It's a bit of an in-joke about how many Claudes were showing up in my stories.

Some years ago, I began writing a silly story on a forum for Super Smash Bros. - I eventually came to realize that what I wrote was part a world known as fanfiction...and I was hooked. Not long afterwards, I made an account here to share my stuff with even more people. I now know that I love to weave tales (both original and based on other work), for my own enjoyment and for those that love to read.

My style or writing mostly encompasses action and adventure, with lots of humor sprinkled about and maybe a little romance here and there too. My Kirby Smash Brothers stories are what I consider my primary series, but lately I've been expanding my stories, and I'm even doing a joint project with some other people!

This collaboration project, called the New Testaments series, will cover the Seiken Densetsu/Secret of Mana series. You can check that out here right now.

I am a fan of constructive criticism - I like to write and receive it. If you see me point out some flaws in your work, don't be offended. It's not a personal attack and I don't hate your story. That goes for me as well; as much as I scour my stories for perfection, there's nothing like another pair of eyes to see what you didn't.

Some stuff about me:

I'm a freelance writer living in America...my interests are Games, literature, some sports, music, writing (a little obvious, right?), drawing, etc.

My favorite game series are, in order: Star Ocean, Seiken Densetsu (Mana), and Metroid. After that, there's too many to list properly. I like all kinds of video games, though I prefer Action RPGs.

I've started putting more time into original fiction in hopes of getting it published. As such, my activity here will probably decrease. I have no plans of abandoning the site, but if things turn out all right, there's no telling what the future holds...

Kirby's Insane Journey is a series of humorous, adventurous Super Smash Brothers stories. The first three can be pretty random, but starting with KIJ4 the series changed direction with a real storyline and better writing that reflected my maturation from a teen with no real writing experience to a young adult. They usually don't deal with tournaments, "smash mansions" and the overdone likes but rather life outside of battles, and whatever threatens the Nintendo universe today. The first and third Kirby's Insane Journey stories have been removed from FFdotnet, however, you can still read them by clicking the links above.

I Like Cars is a poem written by Captain Falcon about his two loves in life: cars and Samus Aran.

Kirby's Insane Halloween is a holiday themed sidestory. While enjoying the Halloween-esque Poe Festival in Hyrule, the Smashers come across a real haunted house believing it to be fake, and get caught between ghosts, pranks, redead, and a fifty year old murder mystery. Kirby's Insane Halloween II: The Cursed Word features the Smashers going to Earth to experience a genuine Halloween, but Yoshi's antagonistic behavior gets him cursed, which has ramifications for the group on their night out when saying the word "Shadoink" (IT'S A REAL WORD) transforms victims into their costume. Dreamweavers - Kirby's Insane Halloween III is the final entry in the Halloween trilogy; the Smashers find their dreams and nightmares seeping into the waking world, jeopardizing their well-being, their mental health, and their worlds. Obscure entities known as Dreamweavers may have something to do with that - as does a malevolent force thought long dead.

Kirby's Heartfelt Christmas is a Christmas anthology featuring stories curated by yours truly about the Smashers incorporating the values of Christmas into their lives after learning about the holiday.

New Testaments series follows up after each Mana games ends, and tells a new tale involving both new and old characters. Each New Testament is organized as an umbrella of projects related to each game.

Star Ocean Zero is an original story in the Star Ocean universe that takes place in 2011 - approximately 86 years before Star Ocean 4. It marks an (believed to be) unrecorded encounter between man and alien as two Earthlings are caught in an interplanetary feud that just may be related to Earth's immediate survival.

Farce Ocean is a the name of a line of alternate novelizations for the Star Ocean series. They tackle 'what if' scenarios that alter the course of the original storyline through various means. Wings to Another Time is a SO3 Farce Ocean adaptation, exploring a scenario where Sophia Esteed and Fayt Leingod trade places early in the game's storyline, a switch that may have occurred when I was haphazardly dumped into the plot by the Ethereal Queen for her own mysterious reasons.

Soul of a Smasher:

Vol. I - Minuet of Link
Vol. II - Nocturne of Samus
Vol. III - ???

Soul of a Smasher is a new Smash Brothers series I'm writing. It is not related to Kirby's Insane Journey. In this series, self-contained worlds exist in a cold, empty space known as The Void, and several of those worlds orbit and rely on the main world, Centropolis. A dark god only known as Azazel threatens to consume Centropolis and its surroundings. Lucario leads a struggling band of warriors in a war against him, only to find the pool shrinking with heroes tragically cut down in separate battles. But the soul cannot be destroyed, and rebirth is the first step to awakening and ascending into a stronger force!

Minuet of Link is the first installment. The Hylian hero meets an untimely death after a brutal battle with Azazel's spawn, a wraith-like demon named Kneph. Reborn in Centropolis, Link leads the simple life of a teenager in high school with vague memories of his past life and the loved ones left behind, but his ignorance is soon dispelled when Lucario confronts him and attempts to unlock his old memories in order to reawaken his soul. Link returns to Hyrule with Lucario, attempting to pick up the pieces of his broken life while preparing for a rematch with the demon that still terrorizes the land's denizens, and it is here where he meets the Smash Brotherhood, a small collection of strange people from many worlds all with the same goal in mind - destroying the coming darkness. Even with the Brotherhood by his side, can Link harness his elemental abilities and save Hyrule from Kneph's indiscriminate massacres?

Nocturne of Samus is the second volume of the series. Samus Crowe is a cop in Centropolis that experiences visceral dreams of another life as a legendary spacefaring bounty hunter, including a particularly chilling encounter with a demon woman calling herself Avira. She soon learns that her dreams may be more real than she realized when she encounters Lucario and the Smash Brotherhood. Meanwhile, Dark Link descends on Hyrule while the Brotherhood is away.

Other Stories:
Final Fantasy Claudus XIII
Final Fantasy Claudus XIII International
Tales of Frue Destruction
Legacy of Evermore

Final Fantasy Claudus XIII is a parody I wrote based on the short FFXIII E3 2006 trailer. I wrote it in the middle of the night after reading too much of the idiotic bashing on the GameFAQ's forum. I needed to vent somehow, so...there's plenty of not-so-subtle potshots at GameFAQs and the stereotypical users that attacked the game based on the trailer. The "Claudus" between FF and XIII is just a parodical way to distinguish it based on the latin fetish SE has displayed with the titles of the other FFXIII games (Final Fantasy Versus XIII, Agito XIII, etc.) It has little to no continuity and plenty of nonsense. Trust me, it's fun and therapeutic to write something like this once in awhile. A parody of the actual full game is in the planning stages and will bear the International label.

Tales of Frue Destruction is a one-shot humor story based off of the Lufia series and details the events immediately after a theoretical third resurrection of the Sinistral of Destruction, Gades. The sinistral attacks a nearby town and seeks to harass a class of school children but gets more than he bargained for.

Legacy of Evermore is a follow up to the underrated game, Secret of Evermore, taking place twenty years later. Elizabeth Ruffleberg returns to the Ruffleberg manor after the death of her grandfather to learn the fate of Evermore, and her students Luke and Krista, and their cat Domino, also get pulled into the investigation. Together, they learn that a lot has changed in twenty years, and it may be connected to the secret origins of Evermore, a secret Professor Ruffeberg never dared whisper near even his own kin.

I've seen people doing them for awhile, and now I think it's time that I also list My Fanfiction Likes and Dislikes:

Likes:
- Good self insertions : one of the first really good fanfics I read was a self insert, despite the warnings to avoid them like the plague
- Humorous stories : Humor is my specialty, and one of my favorite genres to read
- Originality : Something that hasn't been done before, or at least hasn't been done to death
- Helpful reviews : Constructive criticism and real feedback, not just "luv it" or "ur story sux"
- Good Dialogue : This is underrated. Good dialogue makes the characters that much better when they feel so real. I'm working on improving dialogue in my own stories.

Dislikes:
- Mary Sues & Gary Stus : I dislike these with a passion. I don't want to read a story where your character continually bends the universe at their will, accomplishes something the protagonists have been trying to do for as long as who knows what in a matter of chapters, makes all the characters want and love them like bestest friends EVAR within a matter of minutes, etc. This is poor writing in its most blatant form.
- Clueless Sue tantrums : people who do not take kindly to any kind of constructive criticism, especially when involving Sues)
- Flamers : People who are simply here not to write and share their stories with others, but to attack and flame a select few who have the audacity to write something they didn't like
- Praising bad stories : Something most people here have problems with. An absolutely terrible story getting reviews telling them that their story is great, the best thing ever, etc. Come on people, you don't have to lie to them. Telling them that their story needs work and giving suggestions isn't a crime. They aren't going to hang themselves if you don't worship their story in your review. You're not helping them write any better by telling them lies. I believe in helping people improve.)
- Author's Notes that are longer than the chapter : It might sound ridiculous or exaggerated to people that haven't seen it, but I've encountered more than one story where the author has his/her little skits with the characters/muses in the story at the beginning and end of the chapter, only for both parts combined to be longer than the chapter itself.
- Don't like don't read!!! : Terrible mantra. You want as many perspectives on your writing as possible. It's better to learn why people dislike your work rather than insinuating that they aren't allowed to have that negative opinion. On top of that, it's a stupid, juvenile phrase. How does one know that they dislike it...if they don't read it?

All of these dislikes makes me seem like an easily annoyed person...
