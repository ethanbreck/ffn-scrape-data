Author has written 8 stories for Magic: The Gathering, Elder Scroll series, Shuffle!, Yakitate! Japan, Elfen Lied, Ranma, and Familiar of Zero.

After reading some people's profiles I realize that mine is a bit blasse. So it shouldn't hurt to write a bit more about me.

I'm an avid watcher of anime, reader of manga/books, and player of video games. I have aspirations to become a manga-ka or a screenwriter (it might be difficult to tell with my works :D ).

To start on my fanfiction, a little pointers first hand. One, I have, and do write OC fictions (for those unaware of what OC stands for, it means Original Characters, and the worst of the group are more popularly known as S.elf I.nsertions or Mary Sues), although I don't ONLY write OC's (see Elfen Lied and Haruhi Suzumiya fanfic).

Yes, I am aware that a majority of OC's are the author's "avatars" for the story and very few OC's actually relate to the story much less enhance it and actually contribute to the original work. I'd like to think that I can write OC's that people can actually enjoy reading without going "ugh, not another one." But that's not for me to decide, that's for you the readers. I'd appreciate it if one can at least read one chapter of a story with a OC just to get feel for the story, I can't guarantee you'll like it but I myself enjoy making stories, my favorite story right now being Shuffe! Hope as I greatly enjoyed the anime (not often a guy in a harem anime actually chooses a girl). You should be wary of OC's, but not hate them immediately, they might not be half-bad. And hey, what is a CC but the creators OC?

Oh yes, I do not support OCxCC pairings. Just...no.

Second, I write long chapters. Unless specified, a chaptered story (or a one-shot fanfic) will be, at least, 15 pages long. If that's too much for someone in one sitting, lrn2rd. :D Just kidding, but I do write long chapters.

Third, I implore to you, the reader, to point out any mistakes in grammar, continuity, or anything in my stories that is wrong. I realize I am not Stephen King and as such I wish for all reviews/comments/criticisms/etc. Refering back to point one, and this is a big issue, PLEASE let me know if my OC's are taking too much away from the main characters. OC's should enhance the story and its characters, not take away from them.

And now for the stories themselves:
