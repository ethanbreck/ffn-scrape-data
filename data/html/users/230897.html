Yup, I'm back in the fanfiction scene with a new name and a revamped profile. After about four years of having a blank profile with nothing but 'Procrastination Station!' written and a few old favorite stories and authors.

Now I have actual stories and, following, an actual profile. So let's get the party started.

Name: Julia. I also answer to Jello, Jewels, and Julie (but it bugs me when people call me that).

Age: 17

A little about me: I was born up north, but was raised and live in Texas. I really love this state and it will always be my home, even if I live somewhere else in the future. I'm a senior in high school, and loving it. Soon I'll be going off to college at University of North Texas (I've already been accepted!) and I can't wait. I don't know what I'll be majoring in, so I'm just like most freshman, but I have a basic idea.

I love having intelligent conversations with people. My outlooks and ideas are very open and comprised of bits of information I gather from many different sources. I'm always interested in learning about new ideas and debating.

About my writing: I'm mainly a poet, but I do enjoy writing prose sometimes. The main purpose of my starting up again with writing fanfiction is to improve upon my prose-writing skillz. I've found that I'm best at (and enjoy writing) vignette style stories. This is probably because I always have this urge to tell the reader everything right at the beginning of a story instead of drawing it out. So, most of my stories will be in that style revolving around multiple characters.

As for my poetry, I write partly mushy, emotional stuff, like most poets do. But I also write prose poetry, mainly either super creepy or just plain silly stuff. It is important to note, for people who have never met me, that I'm really seen as "that quiet girl". I never really get mad at anyone and I'm constantly smiling. So then, when people read some of my poetry, it totally shocks them, because some of my best poems are twisted fairy tales about the girls in them killing people in creepy ways. I love shocking people like that.

Some things you should know: I'm not a shipper. I hate the way people take shipping so far as to get in arguments over it. There are some ships I enjoy, and some I don't. If it's well written, I'll probably read it. If it fits into my story, I'll probably write it. I find crack!shippings absolutely hilarious. Canon or could-be-canon ships are usually quite dull to me. Ship wars are lame. That is all.

My stories:

Wisdom- Avatar- Story following the lives of (mainly) Katara, Toph, and Sokka years after the war. Aang died in order to defeat the Fire Lord and everyone else went their separate ways afterwards. Warning: there are a lot of OCs in here. Now, I promise you, they are in no way Mary Sues. I wouldn't even have written them, but my story called for it. But if the idea of OCs bug you, then don't read.
(I promise you, I'm not dead. I just haven't updated because of a mix of either being really busy, or just plain lazy. When I do get off my butt, I promise promise promise a very large update.)

Power (tentative title)- Harry Potter- (coming soon) A wildly AU story, where magic takes a different form. Instead of being the same for everyone, it shows itself differently in each person. Hogwarts is a school that helps children discover and train their powers.


Take a Leap- original- (coming soon) A story about two friends and their journey through different lives. Even as they're reborn into different lives and worlds, they'll always manage to find each other.

And now, I leave you with this:

You know it's Baroque if you turn the Handel and it doesn't turn Bach.

(ha)
