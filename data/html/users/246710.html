Rules for A-hole

I'm providing the rules and variations that I play with when playing this game. Try it out at home with friends or family. It's a lot of fun!

1. Whoever deals the cards plays the first hand for the first round. After that, it will go according to who is the A-hole. All consecutive hands are started by one designated as such.

2. Any card can be played in the first hand but each player must play a card (or cards) higher than the previous one. For example: The first player plays a pair of threes. The next player must play a pair higher than threes, such as fours, kings etc.

3. Twos are wild cards. They can beat any hand but there are rules to using them as well.

a) a single two can beat a single card or a pair.

b) two twos can beat three or four of a kind.

c) if playing with more than one deck, then three twos will beat five and six of a kind.

d) four twos will beat seven and eight of a kind.

4. Jokers beat EVERYTHING, no matter how many cards are in the hand just played but two Jokers do not beat a single Joker. Why would you waste them on that kind of a move?

5. Once the positions have been determined, the President will receive the A-hole's two highest cards and the President will give them two of their lowest cards in return. "Lowest" can also mean singles that the President doesn't want to keep in their hand. It's a handy way to make sure that you always have pairs and higher.

6. The Vice President will receive the Vice A-hole's one highest card and the Vice will give them one of their lowest cards in return. See definition of "lowest" above.

7. Once the positions have been determined, only the one designated as A-hole can touch the cards that are played on the table in order to rake them off once the hand is done. Any other player touching the cards forfeits their hand and becomes the new A-hole. The hands are exchanged and gameplay continues as usual until the hand is done.

8. If anyone cannot play a card, they toss a number of pennies equal to the number of cards into a bowl on the table. The winner claims the pennies at the end of the game.

9. The hand only goes around the table once. The player who started the hand cannot end it. It must be finished by someone before them.

10. Once the positions are determined, the A-hole rakes, shuffles and deals the cards for the remainder of the game.

Variations

I had everyone in my story sit according to their position in the game but that is not necessarily a rule that needs to be followed. I only used it to heighten the tension between two characters.

Another variation, for those of you old enough to use it, is as follows. If any player cannot play a hand, they must drink a shot equivalent to the number of cards played. It gets really hard if there are two decks in play since the shots can range up to six or seven at once. Please be very careful if you use this rule. Remember, no drinking and driving and don't make yourself sick just to play the game. Another variation to this is to do one shot if cards cannot be played.

I hope these rules clarify the way the game has been played in my story. If you have any further questions, please do not hesitate to email me and I'll answer them as best as I can.
