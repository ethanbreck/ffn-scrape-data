January 26, 2006

A new year, and new opportunities for stories.

I hope to revise some of my older works, as they’re getting painful to read. I’m also expanding my range, both fandom and genre-wise. My boyfriend, NetOwl, and I maintain a separate profile for collaborative works: LemonSmoothie.

Proposed Works:

Untitled: ( Star Ocean: Till the End of Time) Fayt and the gang receive invitations to a mysterious party.

Untitled: (Where on Earth is Carmen Sandiego?) An Acme detective is killed in the line of duty. Carmen didn’t do it, so who did? Zack and Ivy’s father, Charlie Jenner, attempts to solve the mystery.

Cat’s in the Cradle: Claude finds his father alive. Ronixis has suffered amnesia from the destruction of the Calnus. Claude finds himself torn between building his relationship with Dad from scratch, or to reconstruct the lost memories.

The Beast is Yet to Come: (multiple crossover) Sequel to For Bitter or For Worse. Amok Moonrah, an ancient demon, awakens and terrorizes Agrabah. Worse, yellow journalist Shirley Wright is covering the ADAPT's mistakes -- and airing them anywhere there's television reception!

Untitled: (Darkwing Duck) Morgana is not pleased to learn Darkwing's ex-girlfriend is back in St. Canard. She's even less pleased to find that the ex-girlfriend has been dead for over ten years, and has returned as an angel. Inspired by the wonderful Tim Burton movie Corpse Bride.

In Progress:

Mover and a Shaker: (Lilo & Stitch) Original version was removed because it wasn’t as good as I’d hoped. Introduces Experiment 499, whom Lilo names Carrie after Stephen King’s angry telekinetic prom queen.

Never Too Late: (Gargoyles) My best story in the Gargoyles category, though the earlier chapters are in desperate need of revision. I will finish it first, though.

To Revise:

For Bitter or For Worse: A multiple crossover peppered with laughter and in-jokes. Spans the best of Disney's television history. Revised because I'm very unhappy with how it turned out.

Witless For the Prosecution: (Kim Possible)

Most of my Darkwing Duck works will be redone, especially "Look Before You Leap."
