As of 08/10/2019...

(What got updated?)

It's, uh, been quite a while since I messed with this! To make up for that, I've given this profile the biggest update it's had in ages! Because I made a buttload of stories this year!

Honestly, I have no idea how to explain it. All I did was binge Steven Universe while sitting at home during the month-plus government shutdown, then after reading some really good fics, I was compelled to write my own vision for a Season 5 ending (and finished the first act before Change Your Mind aired) while also wanting to just make a story where Peridot is amazing and properly utilized.

And then I wrote like, around 265k words' worth of this vision in the span of a little over 2.5 months. This fic is much better organized on AO3, for the record, if you'd prefer to read GA with more chapters of shorter length plus visuals. Then came May, and considering I ended GA with a looooot of dangling plot threads that could easily spawn stories following it in this specific continuity, I ended up making 6 more SU stories. And allllll of 'em are Stevidot. Stevidot is a lovely pairing that warrants way more content than it actually has, so I've been compelled to donate to the cause. I'm well aware of the stigma that comes with being a Stevidot shipper, but A: this ain't my first time shipping a massively unpopular and disliked pairing, and B: every reason it's hated is total objective BS.

So, yeah. Needless to say, should you take issue with my preference, don't bother. I'll delete the flames as I won't dignify those with a response. After all, I think Lapidot is morally wrong on all sorts of levels, but you don't see me attacking fans over it. And despite popular belief, it's not sacrilege to not ship Connverse. There's my pre-emptive cautionary statements on the matter (luckily, I've gotten very little harassment... guess that goes with being a latecomer in the fandom!).

I intend on continuing to make stories in the GA continuity in the future, but I'll just let that come about naturally rather than plan for them.

As for Trifecta - no, it certainly is not dead. It's not dead until I say it's dead. I'm a good chunk of the way into Chapter 21, but I haven't made any progress since last August. And after that, I didn't write a single goddamned thing until I was compelled to write Gem Ascension late this January. I've not been this active as a writer in all my 20 years as an author, so I honestly don't know what the fuck is going on. However, I fully intend to resume Trifecta when the time is right. So long as I keep striking while the iron's hot, I can hopefully maintain this writing groove and keep on being productive!

So, wish me luck! Believe me, I feel endless guilt being so relentlessly productive for these SU stories while I haven't touched Trifecta in almost a year, but as I said last time, a break from the fandom is probably what I need to get back into the groove of things. Can you believe I've not yet watched a single episode of the Sun & Moon anime? I'm really out of the loop; I'm barely aware of the goings-on of Gen VIII...

Enjoy some new pairing preferences and story descriptions, anyway! And honestly, I'd stress swapping to my AO3 account for the foreseeable future, as it's much more organized.

So, what can I say? I've been writing fanfiction for years, even before the advent of the internet. Due to my apparent lack of ability to care for anything I create by myself, it's likely I'll stay in fanfiction as far as my writing "career" is concerned. It doesn't matter how old I get; I have very little shame. But I'm a 33 year-old female, if you're that curious. I also have a habit of being rather long-winded with my writing, and the profile is no exception, so if large bios somehow upset you, please be a reasonable, mature person and just hit the "Hide Bio" button hovering somewhere above this profile.

This profile also serves as my reference sheet, kind of. With the fandoms I've been involved with over the years, I find it convenient to record my particular tastes here if I need to reference them in the future. It especially helps if I haven't been involved with the fandom for years and intend on returning. I would say it's highly unlikely that I will actually write for every fandom I'm interested, but I do read for most of them, which might be nice if any other authors stumble into my profile and have written fanfics suited to my tastes. ... It also helps dodging the ignorant requests when I lay out my ground rules. I'm sociable enough, so don't hesitate to contact me if you so desire.

As far as my fanfiction ethics go... well, in addition to the long stories, I tend to lean towards unofficial continuations, AUs, and crossovers. Most likely, there is a lot of drama to be had, but now and then, maybe some adventure or severe psychological experiences as well. I'll do friendship and romance; boy/girl, girl/girl, boy/boy is all the same to me, so I've got broad horizons in terms of pairings. I'll even consider incest. Canon, fanon, or crack also doesn't matter to me; they're all equal and fair play in fanfiction as long as they're written well. And I've seen that anything can be written well just as easily as they can be written badly. That doesn't mean there aren't pairings I don't like, however. I usually have some kind of logic behind what I dislike, so it's rare that I ever change my mind about it. However, I believe all views have a place here, so I tend to ignore what I don't like rather than flame over it.

Same is said for characters, in a sense. I'm not about to pretend to be omnipotent and like every single character ever made. Some are just inherently annoying whilst adding nothing to the story, others were poorly handled in canon, et cetera. At the same time, I'm not about to cry for the cases of character-bashing. Most of the time it's poorly done by the author, but often said author isn't making a good story as it is, so it seems silly to focus on the character-hate aspect. However, I think the idea of defending characters is somewhat ridiculous considering that they aren't even living, breathing people. I'd find it much more healthy to vent my frustrations and hatred over something that's not even alive rather than fixate on a real life person. And believe it or not, I've seen rare cases of character-bashing where it's done tastefully, humorously, and is even well-written and doesn't ruin the story. As for how that translates into my writing, I don't think I've had to deal with a character I outright hated for many years. Chances are, should I come across that situation now, I'd probably have the character written out of the story. That's only for an extreme case, though. Most characters I don't like don't bother me to the point of utter avoidance.

I'm very focused on keeping the characters in-character for every story, though many plot elements may dictate some sort of addition/adaption, which I figure is normal for fanfiction, anyway. The only people I don't really like are purists, as I think they're little more than party-poopers. Thankfully, most true purists probably wouldn't be caught dead on this site... the ones here who claim to be purists are usually the ones with really convoluted ideas about what pairings are canon and such. I don't concern myself with that.

Anyway, my tastes per fandom might be a little unorthodox, so I hope the general public can enjoy what I have to offer! Lastly, my writing spells are somewhat unpredictable, so there might be a time where I'm churning out stories by the chapter, and then there might be months where I go without submitting a thing. I have something of a "I Write When I Feel Like It, No Sooner, No Later" policy, so it's not advisable to be holding your breath for my incomplete works.

Anything else you might want to know? Just ask.
