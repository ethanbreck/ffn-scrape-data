-- Blog Personal--:

3/18/09-- Spring Break!!

Eh, it's not like I have the entire week off...college likes to assign me things to do. One that's been hard for me to work on is my oral presentation for American Romanticism. I have to find an article on an author I've read during the semester, summarize it and argue its methodology, its weaknesses, whether I like it or disagree with it, and how would it infer other students in a research project. I have to write a 3-5 page paper along with this ten minute presentation, and have a rough outline to turn in too...

I wouldn't be whining so much if it was just a paper...I don't like oral presentations too much; although, I just had one last week with my Satan group for Paradise Lost in my John Milton class--we surprisingly did very well, and I did too, not stuttering nervously one bit!! It was great, though I didn't have hardly any sleep that day (I only had an hour, so I was bascially up over twenty-four hours, crazy).

Oh well, I'm going to try to get that sorted today, work on my other classes, and then be done so I can enjoy the rest of my Spring Break before heading back to class next week. I hope everyone else is having a good week.

3/5/09-- Busy busy busy.

College and work always keep me busy--but I AM working on finishing What Happens After Next? Hopefully, if I'm diligent, I can get it done this month. If I do, then I'll work solely on updating Portrait for a while, but also begin working on my next Namixas...which I'm now having a hard time deciding which one I should work on. I just got an idea for maybe which idea I should persue--and you, the readers MIGHT help me decide when I finish WHAN? If I can't decide by then, I'll let you guys help me.

So now, I'm off to work on filling my Creative Writing journal with junkyarding, entries and improvs--and to let you all know, I compiled a Reading Layout file of Portrait of a Young Woman for my Creative Writing professor to look at, just to show him how "unlazy" I've been, if it appears I've been slack on my entries...

Oh, and another thing.

A friend and I are working on a script for a mini-series we're planning start filming next month/over the summer. Right now, the script is titled "Agent of Chaos"--but we're changing it to "Chaos Cause." It's a mystery-thriller about a Joker-esque serial killer who has a peculiar taste for the cinema in his killings...and it's up to a detective in a local precinct to stop him. I just finished writing the first draft of Episode 01, so if any of you are interested in reading and giving me feedback, it'd be much appreciated. Just drop me a line, and I'll probably create a link to the copy...

And I've gotten back to reading Sokais and Namixas, trying to find new, quality fics to add to Recommended Reads in the future. It's great feeling like part of the community again.

So now, it's back to journaling. I hope you all have a great week! Until next time, later.

2/27/09-- Hello everyone!!

I'm finally glad to update Portrait of a Young Woman this week!! It's been forever since that story has seen a new entry, and hopefully with that out of the way, What Happens After Next? won't be too far behind.

For the PlayStation 3 gamers, Killzone 2 comes out today!! It's the much-lauded graphics powerhouse first-person shooter exclusive from Guerilla Games, a second-party developer (I think) from Sony Computer Entertainment Europe, which has been talked out forever...but it's finally out today!! I'll probably end up getting it, as I enjoyed the demo last night, though it was REALLY short...

On the creative writing front, I'm learning on to emphasize dialogue and characterization, and focus less on little words in speaking descriptions that end up "reaching for effect." As my creative writing/poetry professor says, "The reader should be able to pick up on the voice of your characters within a certain context--they can't do that, well--you haven't accomplished your job," among other things.

So, probably beginning in my next Namixas, you'll probably see another "minor" stylistic change to suit these principles. Hopefully it'll make my writing better than it already is (I always want to improve). However, the only thing that gets me, is that I'm "holding back" for that class; I'm not going full force...or at least I feel like I'm not. I'm just so scared that my "true" writing style will be so tightly criticized that when everyone suggests improvements, then it'll take away what makes my writing personality "unique"...at least in my opinion.

Oh well, I should just try it out in my next workshop to see what everyone really thinks...maybe it won't be TOO bad.

So everyone, enjoy my update, leave me a line if you want, and enjoy the weekend!! I'll see you all later!!
