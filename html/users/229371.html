Author has written 1 story for Evangelion.

I am an extremely stressed college student who reads fanfiction to soothe the rabid fangirl beast that lives inside her brain and distracts her from homework. Also, I procrastinate on my own fanfiction like nobody's business. I only become inspired when I want to read that one fanfiction that doesn't seem to have been written yet, so I feel I must take up the task and write it myself. This has led to many, many projects being started and none of them being finished. Including the ritual Mary-Sue story that every female fanfiction author goes through at the age of fifteen. Never finished it. (Of course, that's probably a good thing). I know how frustrated I feel when reading something I honestly like, finishing the most recent chapter and then noticing that the last time the story was updated was five years ago. So my one promise is to never post something unless it is already finished.

In other words, you may be waiting a while before you see anything on here.

Story Summaries

Religion and Evangelion: A User's Guide: There's...honestly no GOOD explanation for this. A while ago, a couple of my friends starting discussing the symbolism in NGE (and believe me, that's a conversation that should never be started unless you're prepared for the eventual bloodbath). A few of my friends actually DIDN'T recognize the religious symbolism in the anime, not knowing anything about the Old Testament and Judaism. So I, who have never read the Bible but have seen a couple History Channel documentaries on the Old Testament, clearly felt myself competent enough to explain it to them. This is, nearly verbatim, what I told them. In an accent not unlike the priest reading the passage on the Holy Hand Grenade in "Monty Python and the Holy Grail". (I would just like to add that there were no questionable substances being imbibed by my friends and I at the time. I really AM just that crazy). I question my judgement in posting this, but sadly, this is the only thing I have actually completed that somewhat resembles fanfiction. My desire to prove some progress in my creative abilities far outweighs my concern about offending people who may not recognize my sad attempts at humor. In that mindset, I have opened a bag of marshmallows in preparation for whatever flames may come my way.
