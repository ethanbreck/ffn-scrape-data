Oh, wow. It is definitely time to update the bio.

Who am I? I suppose that remains to be seen. I shall be very interested to learn the outcome. If anybody has any clues, please let me know. It is, after all, a matter of great personal interest ;)

I know, I know. I don't get any points for prolific writing. I can only hope that quality will go some little way to make up for the lack of quantity. Thank you to all my kind reviewers- there's nothing an author appreciates more than someone who takes time to fully consider their work and provide a competant response.

What am I up to at the moment, you may ask? I'm currently in my first year at the University of Edinburgh, Scotland, studying for the MA in Celtic. And yes, I take my field seriously, thank you very much. My focus will be in vernacular Medieval literature. Oh yes, I'm learning (supposedly) Scottish Gaelic at the moment. A chairadean! What other language could use seventeen letters to produce two syllables and three consonants? You see now why this is an absolute must for my repetoire.

I'm also studying linguistics, and vastly spiraling off into Henry Higgins-dom. I now know exactly what Tolkien meant when he said that 'th' represents a VOICELESS dental fricative, NOT a VOICED one- the voiced correspondant is represented by 'dh'. Ah. Every field has its uses ;)

Current interests of the moment: Harry Potter (ever and always!); symbolism (predominantly Jung, but I'm getting into Joseph Campbell at the moment); Phantom of the Opera (again, an all-time favorite); fantasy and mythology (always, always, always, though I must say that at the moment the mythology seems to be taking presidence over the fantasy); astrology (my new field of interest, which I am wading into with all due caution)

Oh, that brings up an interesting point: What do you guys think about the whole Dan Brown/Da Vince Code phenomenon? Love him or hate him, one can hardly deny the significance of the trend... hm...

Well, there seems to be no escaping it- here comes the favorite authors list, in no particular order: Tolkien; C.S. Lewis; Rowling; Shaw; Thoreau; Jung; Shakespeare; Brian Froud; Margurite Henry (geez, haven't read anything of hers in ages); J.M Barrie; Charles Dickens; Susan Kay; Mark Twain; Yeats; Longfellow; Frost;

Least favorite authors of all time: Nicholas Evans; Robert Jordan; Philip Pullman

Absolute least favorite works of fiction of all time: The Time Machine; A Connecticut Yankee in King Arthur's Court (shudders); His Dark Materials

Best fan fic I've ever read: The Long Road to Damascus, by Morrighan. Brilliant. BUT IT NEEDS AN ENDING! Three hundred or so fanfic-ers are permanently hanging off the edges of their seats!

Favorite anime: Inu Yasha, by far. Kenshin probably in second, with Cowboy Bebop in third. I am currently television deprived, though, so the Inu and Star Trek withdrawl have reached cataclysmic heights.

Favorite Star Trek character: TOS: Spock. duh. TNG: Q Am I the only person in the world who likes Q at all? I think he's fantastic!

Favorite Star Wars character: Yoda

Favorite hobbit: Meriadoc Brandybuck I know, I know- no one but me

Favorite Harry Potter character: Sirius Black, followed very closely by (equally) Severus Snape and Minerva McGonagall. Least favorite HP character: Bellatrix Lestrange. I don't care if Jo blows Harry to smithereans and Voldemort conquers the universe. Just as long as Bellatrix comes to an excrutiatingly slow and gruesome demise.

Favorite M&M: Green

Favorite Muppet: Rizzo the Rat

Favorite classic cartoon character: Speedy Gonzales, followed by Peppy La Pew

Favorite anime character: Sesshoumaru, of Inu Yasha

All-time favorite quote: 'We are such stuff as dreams are made on' - Prospero, The Tempest - William Shakespeare

Myers-Briggs personality type: INTJ

Sun sign: Gemini

Ascendant: Taurus

Moon Sign: Aries
