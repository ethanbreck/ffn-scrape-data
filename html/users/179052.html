12th June 2008

I've been a member here since 2003, and...well, I'm really glad that I joined! So far, I've mainly focused on fanfics on Harry Potter, The Lord of the Rings, The Silmarillion, and a few anime; Flame of Recca, Rurouni Kenshin, Get Backers, Kiddy Grade and Ouran High School Host Club. And I hope to delve into Pirates of the Caribbean fanfiction someday.

I like reading fanfics based on the above categories, specifically the first three. I like reading, writing, drawing and watching tv - as do most people, I suppose! Oh, and I have this great dream of publishing my own, original works one day (I've actually partly written two stories that I hope to finish and see how far they go...but I'm keeping my fingers crossed!) -but we'll have to see about that, I suppose.

Hmm...what else? Oh, as for my penname...well, it contains the name of one of my favourite fictional characters - Sirius Black. I just love him (the character) and, like most fans, was terribly upset with the ending of the 5th 'Harry Potter' book, while (blindly) hoping that he'd come back in the last two remaining books. I guess he did come back in book 7...but, well, it wasn't quite like how I expected him to 'return'. But at least that's something. :-)

A list of favourites:

Tolkien, "Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban" - J.K Rowling (And other Harry Potter books in general, particularly the last two), "Pride and Prejudice" - Jane Austen, "Mansfield Park" - Jane Austen, "Emma" - Jane Austen, "Persuasion" - Jane Austen (Yes, I do like a fair amount of her books...and fictional characters too!), "Angels and Demons" - Dan Brown, "The Odyssey" - Homer, "Eldest" -Christopher Paolini, "The Obernewtyn Chronicles" - Isobelle Carmody, "Mao's Last Dancer" - Li Cunxin, "Atonement" - Ian McEwan, "The Curious Incident of the Dog in the Night-Time" - Mark Haddon, "The Bartimaeus Trilogy" - Jonathan Stroud (I didn't expect to particularly like it when I read the first book, but I ended up loving the series. It certainly was different. Particularly the character of Bartimaeus -his sarcasm and wit were just brilliant- and, later in the second book, the dangerously deranged yet amusing afrit Honorius)
