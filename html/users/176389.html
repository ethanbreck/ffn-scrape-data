I can't think of much to say here but I suppose a brief discription of me is what I'm meant to put? Well, besides being tall, blond and slightly insane.
I'm a huge fan of a great many authors who include Leo Tolstoy (I love War and Peace), Alexander Dumas (The Count of Monte Cristo and the author of the Three Musketeer's novels), Clive Cussler (He's my God), Anne Rice, Cicilia Dart-Thornton, Traci Harding, Dick Francis, Jilly Cooper, Judith Gould, Jean M. Auel, Tolkien, Dean Koontz, Richardo Pinto, Terry Goodkind, Ian Irvine and many more.
I'm also writing a novel of my own at the moment which has been a pet project for years now. This means that when I'm not posting stories here or updating, I've gone back to serious writing. But I love being able to talk to the people who read what I write because I enjoy knowing where I need to improve on my writing.

Upcomming stories:

Of A Day Under A Chestnut Sun.
Synopsis:

The sequal to "Of Arabians and Dancing Swords" has Tahirah happily married to Ardeth Bey and the mother of three children. Their eldest son, the young Ardeth Bey Junior sees a girl around his age in a market in Cairo and is attracted to her. When he meets her for the first time a couple of months later, he finds out that she is the daughter of the English Ambassador. Her name is Chantrea - a Cambodian name meaning Moon - and she doesn't get off to a good start with the handsome young man who is the heir to the Alexander fortune and who her parents are encouraging her to become friends with. However, when Tahirah invites her to go on a trip with the family to New Zealand where Tahirah still runs a horse farm, Chantrea jumps at the change to see some of the famous Alexander Arabians in the hope that she might find a horse suitable to help advance her chances as an international three-day event rider. One thing leads to another and she finds herself terrified of falling in love with Ardeth Bey Junior.

The Elementalist.
Synopsis:

Estelle Dubois is one of a handful of Elementalists remaining in existence. She has a twin brother, Etienne who is a werewolf. During her years at Hogwarts, she and Snape were... kinda friends with undiscussed feelings for each other. However, the behavior of Black, Potter and friends, ends up causing them to fight and therefore "break up". The story is set during Harry Potter's sixth year at Hogwarts. During the summer holidays, Estelle had been attacked by Death Eaters who had been sent to capture her for Lord V. Dumbledore arrives in time to see Estelle using her gift to defend herself and offers her a job at Hogwarts. He doesn't tell her that Severus Snape is the Potions Master or that Remus Lupin has returned as the Defense Against the Dark Arts proffesor. Estelle agrees to take on the job of trying to teach Elemental magic to the students of Hogwarts all the while dealing with conflicting feelings for both Snape and Lupin. It gets worse when she realizes that the Death Eaters have her twin brother and that they are going after her Muggle scientist friend Serafina who has a close friendship with Molotov cocktails. With Snape's help, she brings Sera to Hogwarts but soon realizes that Sera and Snape are beginning to feel something for each other that goes beyond the bounds of a Muggle scientist discussing work with a Potions Master. But when Sera creates a "bomb" that combines powerful potions with Muggle technology which allows for Estelle, Remus, Severus and Sera to rescue Etienne though not without the cost of one of their own...
