My Fanfiction "Rules":
"Rules" about the type of fanfiction I read and write.

1) I don't like main OC's. Minor OC's or evil villain OC's are all I can stand. (And I'm not interested in a back story either)

2) I don't like non-canon... of almost anything. I enjoy AU's but if a story is so far off from the series (aka, characters are are WAY OOC, or some randomly paired off couple), I won't read it. (Or write it)

3) My favorite characters tend to be the main female lead. This may seem a little sexist but they're who I relate to the most. There's only a handful of series where this is not the case. (Ex: National Treasure, Scrubs, Black Cat & Shaman King) If not the main female lead, I will tend to gravitate towards the second main male. (Except Scrubs there... I'm most fond of the main character.)

4) I enjoy relationship stories. Any and all. Even if it's a one-shot with a monologue by one character about themselves, I will always look for and enjoy the implied reactions/thoughts about relationships. From romantic, to friendship, to family.

5) I lean towards canon relationship in shows depending on when/where I tune into the series. Sound weird, no?
-If I start a series at the beginning it will usually be the first main couple (or implied couple) regardless if that couple disappears over time. (Ex: House- House/Cameron)
-If I start a series in the middle, I will attach to that couple. Again, regardless if there's another one. (Ex: Bleach- Ichigo/Orihime)
-If there is no implied couple, I stick with that. I don't make up my own (has to be ABSOLUTELY no hints towards someone) and I don't like it when a relationship is built later on. (Ex: CSI- Grissom/Sara- No!)
-Once I have a couple, I stick with that couple. Through thick and thin, through minor and major hints, through together or with someone else, and through love and hate of the fan community. (Ex: Fans seem to love Grissom/Sara, while they seem to really dislike Ichigo/Orihime, and then they seemed to have moved on from House/Cameron)
-Only once have I broken this rule of relationships. (Ex: Digimon Tamers: Rika/Ryo)

6) This may seem random, being number six (you'll understand for 7 & 8) but I don't like M rated stories. K to T for me. The majority of series I watch fall into this rating range and there's no need to make a K kid series overly dark. (Again, that falls under OOC for me)

7) I enjoy family relationship stories. Not incest. Incest implies graphic happenings. I enjoy trying to understand family better. My ultra-fav is between older brother and younger sister. Or something close to that (Ex: Naruto- Neji & Hinata). Next in line would be Brother/Brother (Supernatural and Prison Break) and/or Twins (Ouran Host Club).

8) I'm all for gay relationships but not those that tend to be so far out of character. So no, I don't like non-canon slash or yaoi. (Unless there's a small hint, even if there is another canon. Ex: Kingdom Hearts) Again, those imply graphic happenings. I'll happily settle for close friend relations between two guys. (Ex: National Treasure- Ben & Riley) I believe that someday I'll be able to read slash without it upsetting me but I have yet to find someone who can write it so well, that it just seems right.

9) I love oneshots. They are quick, all you need to know is the series.
Two things about multi-chapters. #1- I sometimes have a short attention span and multi chapters tend to continue something on and on when it should have ended 5 chapters ago. #2- Many authors (me included) don't finish multi chap stories. I don't want to get into a really good story and then have it never finish. (Such as one story under my favs, 4 years since last update.) Unless you know them in real life, there is no way to be sure that the authors will update. (So they update 10 times within a few weeks, what's to say that it won't be another 3 months before the next update?)
