I would not recommend "Pigeons Plot in Secrecy" because I think it's a waste of time. I only keep it up here for completeness of my fanfiction portfolio. Quite frankly, I think it's embarrassingly bad.

Um... I write. Stories and Websites and Computer Programs Look me up as Pandora's Lost Box in FictionPress.net and please visit my website! I like to think it was a worthwhile way to spend my time.

I'm so sorry to Sweet Nothings Fans. I consider it my most popular story because I recieved the most reviews of that story in the shortest period of time. I am apologizing becaue I'm putting the story on hold. When I write it, it feels really natural and I don't really think about what words I'm choosing or where I'm putting the prepositional clause, and so I am sorry to say that I am putting it on hold. I /did/ warn you all, when I said I have no idea where it's all going and so I'm putting it on the shelf to focus on Thrill of the Hunt which has a simple plotline to which ideas zoom like bees to a sweetly, honeyed flower.

If you want to know how I write, I'll tell you. First I think up plotlines and little bits of dialogue throughout the day, and then ponder them at night, when I'm just about to fall asleep. This is a very unproductive way of falling asleep as I am always having to turn on the light and write something down but my best ideas come when my brain is the most nakedly relaxed. Then I take my little notebook with pirates on it (yes, it actually does) and sit at my computer and type up the ideas. Sometimes I'll write an outline if I get ideas for something far in the future of the story's existence but most often I'll write a scene of the story and build the story around it. That's what I did for "Pigeons Plot in Secrecy". It's built around the chapter "How to Play With the Bad Boys". "Sweet Nothings" is the same way, instead of being in the middle, the first chapter I wrote got such praises that I extended it. "Thrill of the Hunt" was inspired by the coloring of the OotP cover because I loved the coloring and so I set that coloring for their sex, dropped Ginny and Draco in, and they emerged deflowered. "Correspondence" and "Solitude Reversed" are built off of later chapters and I'm struggling with the beginnings of both, trying to get the characters to the point where the later chapters begin. "The Yule Ball" came to me while watching MTV along with another Cho/Harry story that I have yet to begin. I don't know why it came to me then, but a bit of narration slipped into my head (Spoiler:"
I turned to see the young Miss Weasley standing in the doorway, the moonlight turning her gold dress to silver. It didn’t occur to me to cover myself up.
“What are you doing here, Weasley?”
“I don’t know.”
She began to undo her hair, pulling the pins out and letting each curl fall gracefully down her back. She didn’t flinch when I unzipped her, nor when the dress fell to the floor.") maybe it's because I rarely watch television or maybe it was what I was watching. I don't know.
