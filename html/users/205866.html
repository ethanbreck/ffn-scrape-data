Fanfic Theories

I write all of my fanfics from beginning to end before I start publishing so I can avoid anything but an [Ongoing] status, but sometimes things happen.
All of my stories will have one of the following status labels:

[Ongoing] - This fic is being updated on a regular basis.
[Complete!] - This fic has been completed and is no longer being updated.
[Hiatus] - This fic won't be updated until the posted date.
[Inactive] - This fic is ongoing, but hasn't been updated in over three months.
[Paused] - For whatever reason, the story is no longer being written, but it has a fair chance of being resurrected.
[Cancelled]- For whatever reason, the story is no longer being written and it won't be resurrected.

No matter the status, I will answer every question that is e-mailed to me.

My 10 step review theory (that I actively follow):

I) A collective group of readers should never have to to submit a certain amount of reviews before the next chapter is released.
II) If you read a story and like it, leave a review saying so, even if it's only a one word reply such as "Great!".
III) If you read 10k words, or 3 chapters of a fanfic (which ever comes first) leave a review, even if you didn't care for the fic.
IV) Authors should answer questions in the review section only if they HAVE to, but full conversations should never take place there. It drives up the review count, giving an inaccurate impression to potential readers.
V) Authors should respect the readers right to dislike or disagree with something in their story. Fair/Constructive criticism should never be deleted.
VI) If there is in-depth question, it should be e-mailed to the author, not addressed in the review section.
VII) Serious questions posed to the author should always be answered.
VIII) If a question is addressed several times, it should be addressed at the beginning or end of the next released chapter. If the story is completed, an FAQ chapter should be created at the end of the story and labeled for spoilers (if applicable).
IX) Authors should keep readers aware of the status of their story[ies] at all times.
X) Authors should contact another author if they suspect plagiarism, giving the accused author a chance to remove the story. If the accused refuses to do anything, the higher ups should be notified.
