Some "Interesting" Facts about me...

1. I was born in Texas, and currently live here. I graduated in from college in 2007, with a Bachelor of Arts- Majoring in Traditional Visual Arts (ex. painting, drawing), with a Minor in Art History.

2. I love to read, draw and write fan stories. I also play video games (when I have time) and watch movies and anime. I'm presently writing and illustrating a couple of original stories that I'd like to get published someday.

6. My favorite anime would be a tie between Trigun and Yu Yu Hakusho (I got tired of people telling me it was a low-rent version of Bleach). Though my favorite manga would have to be Naruto. (I prefer my my own take on the characters' voices). I read Ai Yazawa's "Nana", Julietta Suzuki's "Kami-sama Kiss" for a while, until Viz's took so long to release them that I lost gradually lost interest. Though I still follow Kaoru Mori's "A Young Bride's Tale".

7. While I so read fan-tanslations (mainly because of slow official releases), I'm a supporter of licensed releases- because, as a fan, I believe that the people who made them deserve to make a profit on their hard work.

8. I'm a HUGE fan of the old-school Robotech series (from 1985!). Please, if you want to see a series that is a testament to the early years of Anime in America and how it ultimately gained mainstream acceptance, then check out all three seasons of Robotech. And if you are (or were) a fan, then check out the official release of Love Live Alive (Released by Harmony Gold, A&E, and Lion's Gate). It's a love letter to the series and the fans.

I'm a hopeless romantic at heart, so you'll see a lot of romance and romantic themes in my stories. I've worked hard to show that, as a man, I can write quality romance-based stories as well. I'm also willing to admit that I like to root for the underdogs in the things I watch/read. I admire people who fight for a just cause. I like to think of myself as man of principles... That being said- I place a lot of value on honor, loyalty to family and friends in my writing.

Still, I will admit that I do enjoy a good bit of villainy from time to time. If written correctly, the villain/antagonist can really make a story. A well-written villain can make a story better by reinforcing (through opposition) the agenda of the hero. A prime example of the clash between good and evil is the superbly realized moral conflict between Vash and Legato in the Trigun Anime. It's rare to have such contrasting personae- the gentle and humane Vash versus the cold and nihilistic Legato. Another good example is in Yu Yu Hakusho, in the Dark Tournament, when they're fighting the Shinobi: just when it seems all hope is lost... my main man Kuwabara steps forward to fight. As Koenma says, "There comes a time when a man has to take a stand for what he believes in."

Since 2002, I've written over 40 fan-stories for various anime/manga, comics, and video games. Even now, I have no plans of quitting, so look forward to more stories in the future. And so, if you guys have some time- give my stories a read. Hopefully, you might just like them... perhaps even enough to leave reviews or send me some feedback about what I'm doing right and what I could do better.

Thank you!
