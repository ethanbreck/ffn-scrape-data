Hello! I'm Yuki (also Zen).

I am a teenager from Montréal. Yes, I speak French... I apologize for my poor English writing skills. I have taken ESL (English Second Language) for most of my schooling years and I despereatly try to improve those skills each day. I also apoligize for my Quebec French idioms. If anyone who is not familiar with Montreal slang reads them they might be confused.
Eg. "Ça fait dur!"- It makes hard. This is not a good translation. The expression means that something is not adequate or simply looks, sounds horrible.
"Mets-en!"- Put Some! It's meant to emphasise a point already made. Also a poor translation.

okay thank you...

Since this section is called Bio, I'll talk about my wonderful self. Usually I have a lot to say but nothing comes to mind when I must describe myself.
I will not give any physical description because it's useless. Okay so here goes, writing experience. I write for my school paper. I mostly write about movies, long reviews if you want, but I am currently working on a Lord of the Rings piece that has more to do with the whole J.R.R. Tolkien universe thing than the movies. I also write about our schools events, but dances and parties can only get more boring to write about. They go something like this. Name of event. Time of event. Location of event. Rules that must be followed while participating in Event or face the consequences. Anyways it gets horribly dull after a while. I've also participated in several writing competitions. None of my entries have been sucessful ( I wonder if that means I'm a poor writer? Why of course not! I'll just keep on truckin'...) The latest contest went horribly. But that was because less than 30% of the people in my class read my story. Our teacher gave us 4 days to read 33, 4 to 5 page long stories that centered mostly around the same idea. And mine was number 29. Not at the end so that the people who took pity of the last person would read it but almost. All the people in my class who's stories got selected to be sent off to the real contest were in the top 15. Seriously. I later asked some of the students and some said.(and I quote)
"Well, I never really got around to reading past the second one. (Would you imagine that #1 got the most votes! then #5, #8, #10 and #12)( I know this because I read every single story)(plus these are my friends' stories so they rub it in my face all the time.) So I'm sorry, I really just voted for the first one and the stories my friends wrote (we were allowed 5 choices) Isn't that horrible, I still fume about that.

Okay so that's enough, or so I hope. Version française bientôt!
