On a side note:

Mars on fire brought a good question to my attention. She put it as:

"Socs attack girl, gang rescues her and asks her to live with them" And no, I am not about to bitch about getting flamed. After all, she DIDN'T flame me, she brought up a good point, and I'd like to clarify on what the original intention behind this was.

First off, think about it. The Socs are notorious for jumping the greasers. I seriously doubt that the greaser girls were exempt from this treatment. It probably wasn't as bad as what would happen to the boys, but it most likely did happen. My first intention was to just have the Socs scare her. In chapter three, Pepsi goes into more detail about her life before she was sent back. Since she was abused by her step-father, how do you think she would react to being attacked? She panicked and fought back. Originally, the Socs in that chapter WERE just trying to scare and harass her, and it worked; just not how they were expecting it to. The part with the knife and the threat was supposed to make her just be quiet. They weren't going to kill her.

As for the gang rescuing her, in the book (I think) it states that the gang would usually hang out at the Curtis' house after school/after getting off of work. That was around the time that Pepsi arrived. And let's face it, any nice guy would try to help out a girl who is screaming her head off in fear. Thus, how they came to rescue her.

Then, there's the whole point of Pepsi ending up living with the Curtis brothers. At first, she was only going to stay with them for a few days until they could figure out what to do with her. Once they found out how she came to 1963, they decided to keep it a secret to keep her safe. In chapter three, it states that she did get a job to help them out with bills, groceries, and other things that were needed around the house. In a future chapter, it goes into more detail about how and why she got the job. I'll tell you this - she refused to be a freeloader on the Curtis brothers when they had voluntarily taken her in when they could have just put her in a girls' home and forgotten about her.

I hope that this explains some of the ideas behind the first few chapters. If anyone wants me to, I'll post a one-shot going into more detail about the first few days after Pepsi end up in 1963.
