I mostly read Snape/Hermione fanfiction, but on occasion I do wander into other pairings and works.

Fanfiction is something that I don't even make an attempt to write, nor do I usually make comments on it.

My views on grammar and writing are on the rather uncommon side, so I just prefer to say out of it (ex. I despise The Elements of Style.) altogether. I think it could be mostly summed up in the phrase: All things are possible, but not all things are acceptable. That is, a writer has the freedom do anything they wish when it comes to writing fanfiction, but if they are going to do something it has to be believable in its context. For example, a writer can make Snape get the girl, move to Hawaii and become a surfing instructor. This is all possible. The question then, is not, 'is this a good story?' to me, but it becomes, 'is this really a fanfiction?'. Some times it is really, really is a fanfiction. Other times I think it is simply an author with a good original idea who is perhaps too shy to make the leap into original work.

I could expound on that idea all day, but enough with that.

Lately I've enjoyed translating manga, playing video games (rarely, during the school semester though. :( ) and cooking from kyou no ryouri. I am studying to sit the level 3 Japanese Language Proficiency Test in December. Coffee is something that I love. Doing laundry is something that I hate. Adventure eating is fun, and being lifted suddenly onto my boyfriend's shoulders is terrifying.

Oh, and I am working on getting a Master's degree in Library and Information Science from SJSU right now. It does keep me out of trouble. ;)

I think that almost wraps it up.

Yay totoro!
