Kitriana has been running amok on the internet for a little over a decade, having first made her home on a now-extinct chat room called The Force, where several of her original characters came from, with permission from the owners of their names and personalities. When The Force was killed, she moved on to Cloud City Cantina, D'lan'teria'l, and The Holocron (now called Gal-Com). She has a deviantArt where she places most of her original fiction - those that she doesn't intend to finish or publish. She also has LiveJournal, FaceBook, and MySpace accounts, but will not share these with the general public.

She claims, perhaps jokingly, to have been writing short stories since the day she was old enough to pick up a pen and write more than "The dog jumped over the cat" in her 'Learn the Alphabet' book. She has always enjoyed books, specifically those in the Sci-Fi and Fantasy genre, though she has been known to occasionally pick up classic literature. While she enjoys her artwork, especially her photography, it is her writing that she enjoys the most. Poor spelling and grammar make her cringe, and now that she has learned the proper formatting techniques, seeing their abuse makes her wince.

Kitriana has only one muse, an interesting being by the name of Siria, which she says means "sun-bright", which actually makes sense. The muse has two forms, a feline-esque, and a human. Amusingly enough, Siria is, apparently, an herbivore, and really likes tangy fruits, such as lemons, green apples, and plums. She upsets easily, but is generally a fun-loving muse who likes to irritate Kitriana.

The two make an excellent team, and often work with others on RolePlay sites where advanced writing is preferred, and in some cases, required. Kitriana writes (both fanfiction and RolePlay) in Star Wars, Star Trek, Harry Potter, Stargate, and, of course, her original ideas.

She has recently begun writing fanfiction again, and so dug out her old account so that she could post it on the vastness of the Interwebs. A six year old account that obviously needed to be swept free of cobwebs and redone to update the times, and now here it is, steadily changing so that you, the reader, can enjoy the silliness that is fanfiction.
