NOTES:

Updating Process: I am a very, VERY slow updater. I mostly read other people's work, critique when I can, but I don't get much inspiration from anything, so it hits me very randomly and very rarely. I am also a full time college student and a conceptual artist in training. I have homework varying all the way from computer programming, essay writing to art projects that require ten hour minimum work time, so fan related stuff will always come second.

Reviewing Process: I am mostly a recluse reviewer. I don't review stuff I (a) think is really, really good and doesn't need further assistance, (b) stuff I think is beyond saving and not worthy of my time. (c) Stuff that is a complete and total JOKE! Sometimes, I will review stuff to give new artists a helpful boost (when you are starting out, encouragement can be the most powerful method of help, too much of it will result in hubris, so be careful with it), but most of the time, if I see a problem that is really distracting, damn it, you are going to know about it.

Pairing Policies: Every pairing is a good pairing... if written well and directly corresponds to the content its based on. I HATE things that are one hundred percent out of character (can stomach twenty percent, but not much higher than that), and will often not even bother to review them. (There are plenty of fandom nazis on this site that will be happy to do that, NOT me)

Slash Policies: It's fine. Like I said, every pairing is good if written appropriately. GirlxGirl and GuyxGuy, no problem whatsoever! If you are passionate about something, you write it! Me, I am for gay rights, I voted no on prop 8 in california, and cheered when that proposition was overturned by the federal government. I am not gay (having no interest in any relationship, whatsoever, gay or straight), but I have plenty of friends and even family who are wish the best for them all.

Community Policies: My opinions on what you write don't matter that much, and honestly, I don't really care about that from the get-go anyway. This is the internet: it is a free place for any kind of train of thought, and people can enjoy it on whatever level they want. I aint gonna stop you (aka: report you) unless you really need a wake up call, however... don't you DARE come crying to me because I am (a) working with a pairing that you don't like (b) adding plot elements that have been overused... this is the twenty-first century, NOTHING is original anymore. Avatar isn't original, Harry Potter isn't original either, and the sooner you fan-tards get over that, the better. If there is one thing I hate, it is people blatantly telling me what I can and cannot do.

Author Relationships: Depends on the type of author. Mary-Sue authors don't bother me that much, they are employing free speech, even if they are using it in a terrible way. Fan-brats, annoying as shit and are tenacious parasites, but they are like bears. You stay away from them and they will (most like way) stay away from you. Fandom nazis? Honestly, I used to be one of them (for the Yu-Gi-Oh fandom, anywho), but then I saw how they functioned on other levels and it almost turned me off completely from fan fiction. Honestly, the combination of tolerance (accepting who other people are), acceptance (listening to what other people have to say) and a helpful attitude (willing to help and work with others) make good authors here. Dogmatic takes (thinking less of other people because they don't write the way you want them too), narrowmind-ness (believing your way is the only way) and immature personalities (thinking you have to spread the word on your thoughts and beliefs--seriously, you are embarrassing yourself!) do not.

Final Notes: Everyone has to be treated fairly on this website. Couples, story-lines and fandoms are like religions to some people; Treat them like that.
