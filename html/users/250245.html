I thought that this news article “More Profit with Less Carbon” was very idealistic. Dr. Lovins, the author of this article, kept repeating that in 2050 if the government was to take the right choice and convert to renewable energy sources, then the rest of society will follow. But, firstly, I do not think that our government’s top priority is the environment.. Our government is more concerned with spreading democracy and capitalism than worrying about the environment and the amount of carbon we are pumping into the atmosphere. Lovins’ states that utilizing renewable resources would in the long run be cheaper than using fossil fuels such as coal and oil as our main source of energy, but these resources are costly in the beginning. Another point to take into account is the fact that even if we are to start putting more hybrid models and new “lighter-but-stronger” cars on the market there will still be old cars on the roads that use fossil fuels. We will still need to keep refineries and oil fields and petroleum plants because oil will still be in demand. Another aspect to take into account is the idea of Supply and Demand. At this current time the demand for oil is increasing every day and the supply is slowly running out, but until we do eventually run out of oil most people will not take action in implementing these new ideas. As stated before if fossil fuels are still available than America and the rest of the world will buy these energy sources and until we run out society will not think of finding and utilizing other resources. Most people feel that if it does not affect them now, than it never will. That is why I feel that Lovin’s article is to idealistic to actually come into effect at least at the dates he predicts. Until we absolutely need to start finding new resources America and others are fine with using fossil fuels as resources even though they are destroying our environment, rising in costs, and running out.
