Author has written 1 story for Harry Potter.
I'm a 16-year-old girl from New Jersey, 30 miles from New York. I'm a history geek, fascinated with mythology, and obsessed with books. Among my favorites (Yes, they're in order of preference!):

1. HARRY POTTER!!! No, not the BOOKS per se though
since they are the beginning of the Potterverse
they deserve a certain amount of
reverence...but the fanfic is, in my opinion,
the most captivating on the Internet. I don't
believe in ships since I can see more than one
pairing for each character, but if I did I
would probably be a Draco/Harry shipper. *sighs
as the "stick up their arse" conservatives back
away in horror* They're just...so...
PARADOXICAL! In terms of my favorite
characters, they are as follows:
a. REMUS LUPIN!!! THE ROMANTIC AND DASHING
WEREWOLF!!! Yes, I'm in love...but the
feeling's mutual, right, Remy? (Remy:
*grins and nods*) I tend to appreciate the
Marauders and their time more than
Harry's, possibly because things aren't as
spelled out so there's wiggle room. The
obvious exception to this is, of course,
my D/H obsession. Remus is my favorite
because I see him as the quiet, reasonable
one behind James and Sirius' shenanigins.
He is the poet, the shy one to whom the
world has been harsh. He knows loneliness
and thus knows how to stand being alone,
yet he dreads it. And yet his very nature
forces this solidarity, for he experiences
something that few others can fully
understand...it has changed him, made him
more mature and deep than the rest. *sighs
and shakes her head* I'm boring you,
aren't I?
b. Draco Malfoy. Yes, Ron has officially been
bumped down to third...though if I had to
have one as a friend Ron would beat Draco
any day...but that is for the next little
rambling...Draco is just an incredibly
fascinating character. We know nothing
except that he is deemed evil (or at least
a jerk), but that this is really his
father's infulence. So what you have is a
potentially vulnerable kid under the
facade of a cold-hearted Death-Eater-In
Training. Just THINK of the
possibilities...As a side note, you'll
notice that Harry didn't make the list
though D/H is my favorite pairing. In a
strange brain twist, though I interpret
Draco as much more than JKR made him out
to be, I interpret Harry exactly as she
portrays him: a 2D good guy. His part in
my mind is only as a dramatic foil to
Draco. He is perfect for the role, which
is why the pairing works out so well. But
give me a 2D Harry without Draco and I
lose interest entirely.
c. Ron Weasley has interested me since the
first book. He started out as my favorite
character, then was demoted to second when
the third came out, and is now at third
after my discovery of D/H. The thing that
I like about Ron is that of Harry and Co.,
he is the only normal one. I love using
him because he is the one that has the
human reactions to things, and since he
doesn't come with baggage (ex. books,
scars, dead parents and a sucky home life)
it's easy to gague a situation based on
how he reacts. I see Ron as a typical guy
from my town: a nice, good-looking,
lovable...homophobe. Sadly, normal.
Honorable mentions go to Sirius Black and
Severus Snape, very obviously for completely
different reasons.
2. Animorphs! Yes, the series is (sadly) over...I
loved the ending until the last chapter, where
KAA left us hanging in some random galaxy. I
still haven't forgiven her for that. Anyway, my
favorite character is Tobias and my favorite
pairing (NOT A SHIP!!!) is Tobias/Rachel.
Interesting, that my two favorite book
characters both become animals (not just
MORPHING even in Tobias' case!)...That's
actually sort of disturbing now that I think
about it. Tobias is so angsty and paradoxical
that the competition is not even close...though
the runner-up is Rachel...And as for the
pairing, it is the most fascinating one I have
ever read before. Tobias and Rachel are in
themselves paradoxes, but when they get
together it's almost too mind-boggling. Tobias
has experienced his paradox in a very physical
way...he is a hawk with the mind of a boy. But
he, in the end, accepts himself as he is and
manages to unite the two halves. Rachel,
perfect on the outside, experiences terrible
inner conflict and fails to unify her two
sides. Her death was really the only option in
the end. Their love is born from this
similarity, even though it cannot be seen
easily through their complex differences. It is
also interesting that in the altuniverse of
Megamorphs 4, Rachel, without the war, is
paired with Marco. This helps prove that it is
her paradox, born from the war, that causes her
to go with Tobias.
3. Everworld would have tied with Animorphs had it
not had such a horrendous ending. How I see it,
KAA simply decided that Everworld wasn't worth
her time and scrapped it. Don't get me wrong, I
love her stuff, but HONESTLY. David is my
favorite character, since he's so angst-ridden
and paradoxical (sensing a pattern yet??).
Jalil is a very close second. April is an
unredeemable loser. Christopher, who is
basically a carbon copy of Marco, is the
proverbial class clown character. I appreciate
him but don't really like him. The story had
overwhelming promise, which is why I still love
it, but failed miserably by ending WAY too soon.

I am currently working my way through the Remnants series, which is too young to pass judgement on just yet. So far, 2Face is grabbing my attention more than the others. We'll see. I am also a huge fan of books that explore individual rights and liberties -- 1984, Brave New World, and Farenheit 451 are among my favorites.

My current work, Hope, is getting a break. In the middle of it I discovered D/H and became distracted. I am working on a new chapter which will be uploaded soon-ish. I fiddled with rearranging the pairings but decided that it was all for the best. Expect D/H and maybe some other stuff. That's all for now!

~Gryffin
