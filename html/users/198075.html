Response to reviews for WHLS Chapter Two

Greetings:

Well, where to begin… First, and foremost, I want to thank everyone for their continued interest in all my stories. The overwhelming and continued support sent my way through reviews, PM, and e-mail means more to me than words could ever do justice.

Second, let me offer my humble apologies for going Dark for as long as I have. The recent amount of time between updates has been long and bordering on excessive and I apologize. My reasons are my own but I will simply say that they are of a personal manner pertaining to family and leave it at that.

Third, I would like to attempt, in as non-offensive and belligerent a manner as I can, to explain, or even remind, exactly where Fanfictioncomes from. It comes from the imaginations of men and women, boys and girls, of many levels of skill and technique who take time away from their everyday lives to create the stories we all enjoy reading. Lives full of responsibilities such as friends and family, work, and school, amongst countless other things.

But, one must also remember that Fanfictionis a passion for some, a hobby for many, and a passing interest for most. But, for all, reader and writer alike, it is free entertainment; entertainment that costs the reader nothing to consume and the writer their time to create.

So, please, to end this exposition, I ask that we all take the time to show a little understanding and patience for our fellow readers and writers. Allow them the time to create without being hounded, the time to develop as a writer without being belittled for poor spelling and grammar, and the time to discover themselves as a storyteller without having their ideas and plots being ridiculed.

We all had to begin somewhere and we all can stand to improve. I will be the first to admit and accept my faults. And, it’s only with time and diligence that those faults will hopefully lessen. Allowing me to become the better writer, I’m sure, we all strive to become.

Not to say that I don’t understand the plight of the reader, who’s left hanging, wondering about the status of their favorite stories, or haven’t experienced that nagging fear, after weeks or months without update, that your favorite fic may never be finished. Trust me; I’ve begun reading many a story, which held so much promise, only for them to be left unfinished with me being more than a little annoyed with its author.

And, yet, it’s part of the risk we run, and should maybe expect, whenever we begin reading an unfinished fic. Is that particularly fair, no, probably not? But, if one wants to, it could be seen as the price of admission for us the reader. We offer up our support and hope of an entertaining, completed fic in exchange for a short escape from our everyday lives, an escape brought to us at the cost of the author’s time, patience, and creativity. A fair trade…? That’s for you to decide.

In closing, my thanks and apologies to all who’ve read this. My thanks to those who agree, disagree, or are indifferent to it. My apologies to anyone who may have been offended by what they’ve read as that was not my intentions.

Love & Peace ~ DsirinWsdm

Character Bios & Other Important Facts:

Taki (Tenten’s Mother):

It seems as if in every fic, Tenten is either an orphan or the daughter of a former ninja turned weapon shop owner who’s always male. So, I decided to spin that on its head and feature her mother instead. I’m a big Tenten fan, so I really loved writing this follow-up to her appearance in Chapter Six. Especially, since we don’t know her family situation in canon this made creating one for her all the more fun.

As for her mother’s design, she looks, fights like, and is named after the female ninja Taki from the Soul Edge/Soul Caliber series of video games.

Kakashi’s Father:

This came to me as a result of Naruto’s initial encounter with Tenten in Ch6. And, someone asking in a review what was the story behind the “cursed sword.” When I decided to go back there and introduce Tenten’s mother, Taki, I just needed to mention that sword. Then I started thinking of the different swords that have appeared in Naruto and I decided to use the sword I find to be both the simplest in design while remaining one of the coolest. That led me to Sakumo Hatake and his White Light Chakra Saber. Then the rest of that section developed from there.

The Hakkyokuken (Eight Extremities Fist):

This is the name for Naruto’s revision of the Jyuuken, co-developed alongside Hiashi. I drew my inspiration for the style from the real Chinese martial art. It’s called Bajiquan and Hakkyokuken in Japanese and both come to mean Eight Extremities Fist, I recommend looking it up on Wikipedia.

Versions of the style can be seen in use by Akira Yuki from the Virtua Fighter series of video games, as well as Kokoro from Dead or Alive 4.
