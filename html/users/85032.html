Author has written 14 stories for Star Wars, Harry Potter, and Lord of the Rings.
Hey! I'm a fourteen-year-old southern girl who loves Star Wars, Harry Potter, and Lord of the Rings. I also watch Star Trek (TNG only), but anyway...I hated "Star by Star," thought AOTC could have been better, and haven't read Rebel Dream or Rebel Stand (can't find them! ARGH!) I also think Ewan McGregor is hot, Hayden Christensen is hot, Orlando Bloom is hot, Elijah Wood is hot, and Dominic I-don't-know-his-last-name is hot. Consequently, I love the characters Obi-Wan, Anakin (Solo *sob* and Skywalker), Legolas, Ron, and Sirius Black.

And now, my ships:

Harry Potter-- RON AND HERMIONE FOREVER! Any other pairing involving either of them is just WRONG. Harry and Ginny make a cute couple, and I've been trying to write a romance with Sirius, but I lost the disk with the story on it DARN IT.

Star Wars-- *cringes* I like Jag! DON'T HURT ME die-hard Zekk fans. I like Zekk too. But in the books he's become rather wishy-washy. So, Jag and Jaina, Tenel Ka and Jacen (is Danni dead?), Tahiri and Anakin (WAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH!), and all the married people too (Mara/Luke, etc.), and oh yeah, I love the Rouge Squadron books, so anything involving Wedge/Iella, Tycho/Winter, and Corran/Mirax, is wonderful too. But besides romance anything with Rogue Squadron is good. And anything involving Mara as the Emperor's Hand is cool, too.
I wrote a Obi/Padme story, but that was before I read the AOTC book (go read it now!) and so I don't quite know where that I am with that (Padme/Ani or Padme/Obi or maybe I'll just do both!). But poor Obi should get a girl. Really. I don't know who, but he needs to get a girl.

I don't write Lord of the Rings because I...can't really come up with anything. I suppose I could write something taking place during The Silmarillion, but after RotK all the Elves leave, and they're sort of my favorite. I'm also writing some Original stuff, so watch for that.

Please don't flame me! Constructive criticism, yes. Flames, no. And review!
