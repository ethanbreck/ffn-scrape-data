And now on to the Commandments.

My name is Airlea, and I am a chronic flamer. Well, this is not entirely accurate. I am merely what you might call a Tolkien 'purist' when it comes to fanfiction. Book Canon is Law in my mind when it comes to the fandom. I understand that sometimes stories must deviate from the beaten path in order to illustrate a certain point about a character. I readily acknowledge that it is not possible to follow the canon letter by letter. This is not the issue at hand, however. Here are the Commandments of Book Canon, as perceived by Airlea in regards to Mary Sue - Lord of the Rings fics:

1 - There are no women in the Fellowship. Hence the word 'Fellow'. There are only nine members in this 'Fellowship', as this number coincides with the number of the Nazgul. Nine walkers against the Nine Riders, as it were. There is no way for you to get around this rule without severely deviating from the canon. Any story which contains a female lead who undermines the importance of the Quest is known as a "Mary Sue". If you find yourself writing such sacrilege, do not post it on a public archive.

2 - Characterization is your friend. Read the books to gain insight and understanding into the characters and their personalities. If you are writing the aforementioned Mary Sue and will not be dissuaded, do not turn your male character of choice into some simpering brat. Although you are trying to make your Sue look good by comparison, you are completely bastardizing the noble characters Tolkien wrote into existence. Have you no shame?

3 - Check to see if your Sue can do one of the following - resist the temptation of the Ring of Power; wield a mysterious weapon which no one excepting herself can understand; change her eye color at will; enchant half of the male characters, even those who are betrothed (read: Aragorn), despite her obnoxious personality and overbearing nature regarding her past. If you have answered yes to any of these questions, you are well on your way to completely destroying a magnificent work of literature with your deplorably unoriginal fantasy.

4 - If your Sue has any of the following - beauty beyond any the members of the Fellowship have seen; eyes that are, for all intents and purposes, multicolored (read: her silver-blue eyes with glints of green shone with tears); hair that is more than one color; wings; a mystical weapon that may triumph over the power of the Ring; combat skills exceeding those of Aragorn and Legolas; a tragic past involving betrayal, a broken heart, or murder; an innate hatred of all things male. These are signs that your story is pathetic and you seriously need to rethink your writing. If you have answered yes to any of these points, please do not post your fic on a public archive.

5 - If you, the writer, have not read any of Tolkien's works...If you are a fan of the movie only, you have no right to be writing for the Lord of the Rings fandom. If you are inventing an ending to the story, cease and desist with the abomination! Tolkien already wrote a beautiful, moving conclusion to his epic. You have no right to try your hand at it, especially if you are completely unfamiliar with the plot beyond The Fellowship of the Ring.

6 - Never, never apologize for your ignorance. Never apologize for inexperience. These are both things that can be cured. Stupidity is something you ought to apologize for. Arrogance, conceit, immaturity...These are things you may apologize for.

7 - Do not become indignant and haughty because you have received reviews berating you for destroying Middle Earth and its inhabitants. Learn from those who review your story - and not just those who give positive feedback.

8 - If you are displeased, do not take it upon yourself to attack an individual for the sole reason that they did not tell you what you wanted to hear. Everyone wants their stories to be liked; however, if you do not take steps to ensure your writing is quality, no one will like it. This is a fact of life.

9 - Do not post a story that you are writing for yourself. If you say that the only reason you are writing this is because you want to, to please yourself or a close friend, it does not belong on fanfiction.net. Let it remain hidden from public view on your hard drive. Save the bandwidth - this server is overcrowded already.

10 - Use proper grammar and spelling. Always, always, always! There is no excuse for you to be confusing words such as "their, there, they're" or "your, you're". Run-on sentences are an unforgivable evil; fragments are worse. Lack of capitalization shows inexcusable laziness on the part of the author. If you do not even attempt to fix this, you are clearly not mature enough to write. Use a spellchecker - if your spellchecker is broken or you just can't be bothered to turn it on, hire a beta reader. A friend, a fellow author - anyone with grammar/spelling skills superior to your own.

I've said my piece. If you cannot follow any of the basic criteria I've outlined above, I suggest you have mercy on us purists - cease writing your Mary Sue fic. Remove any others you may have authored from fanfiction.net. You'll find that Airlea is not quite as much a bitch as she might be - my first review is my opinion on the story alone. If you continue to ignore all the advice you've been given, then I really will become a chronic flamer.

Please direct any and all hate mail to hobbit_lass@yahoo.com Have a nice day.
