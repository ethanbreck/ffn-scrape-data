Furor's Lil' Blurb Spot (10.24.2008)

All righty, the next four chapters of Abstain Ye Not are posted. My beta is swamped between school and work, so these are the unbetaed chapters. When she gets a chance, she'll take her time and do them properly, and I'll reupload them, as all shiny and new. Think of these ones as diamonds in the rough. But why make someone wait when there is goodness to be read. Tantalising goodness! And if more people than I suspect are reading this, please don't innundate me with messages and or emails. I have a plot outline, I'm sticking to it, and it will be awhile before Harry and Lucius are even within twenty feet of one another. Please don't ask for the "good stuff" to happen soon it won't.

And I'm not able to squeeze in much writing since the store is in full Christmas swing... which means people call in sick (since booking a day off in advance is simply blasphemy) and leave others to cover their shifts. People like me, who make a killing in overtime pay. Not that the company likes it, but this is what happens when you hire flakey people. My pocketbook is happy, but my social life and writing do protest much. =(

Favorite Saying: "Opera is when a guy gets stabbed in the back and, instead of bleeding, he sings." - Ed Gardner
Favourite Song: Twilight of the Thunder God - Amon Amarth: This is the title of the whole damn album too, and it's all good. Epic even. Hell, even the cover is epic: Thor has Jormungr by the tongue with one hand, and his other hand is holding Mjolnir and ready to strike. Totally epic and totally worth the money paid.

Currently Reading:
Online: Harry Potter and the Nightmares of Futures Past - S'TarKan / Team 8 - S'TarKan / The Marriage Stone - Josephine Darcy: I'm reading 'Nightmares of Futures Past' for the first time; 'Team 8' and 'The Marriage Stone' I'm re-reading again because they're that well-done and classics in my opinion.
Offline: Blindness - Jose Saramago / The Picture of Dorian Grey - Oscar Wilde: Blindness is quite good, but the punctuation threw me for a loop. I'm not used to seeing so many commas. And I just love the tale of Dorian Grey. Oh morality...

Currently Playing: Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney - Also known as Gyakuten Saiba as my friend is apt to frequently remind me. And every so often I'll bellow out, "Objection!" when my manager isn't making any sense. ...I'm sure the man thinks I am truly insane.

Recently Bought: The Tales of Beedle the Bard - Oh yes, I finally pre-ordered this one. I can't wait to see what wizarding folktales are like from a first-hand source rather than the secondary ones we get from the Harry Potter books. I don't think it's possible for me to sound like a bigger dork.

Recently Saw: The Host - I love this Korean horror movie, so I watched it again... for the seventh time. It's a pure throwback to when monster movies had good plots, great characters that you became emotionally vested in and were just plain fun to watch. Not to mention that everything made sense and was neatly resolved by the end of the movie. I might go see Ghost Town soon, but I'm not too sure.
