Picky, Picky, Picky
Verbose, once you get me started
Analytical

Those are three things that you are likely to be subject to if I review.
If I review I either liked your piece very much, but had some questions, or things that struck me with discord. OR you have done something I just find so horrendously wrong I can't not say something (these are few and far between.)

I am also a Purist,
Either an optimistic Pessimist or a pessimistic Optimist (likely a product of being in BOTH Girl and Boy Scouts.)
A procrastinator,
Slightly hypocritical in select issues,
Argumentative (I would like to debate, but few try to reason out their responses)

I can also be self-righteous, but after I burn my ire out, I usually am repentant, for my tone if nothing else.

My preferences for fanfiction:

A strong canon base -
There are many excellent series out there yet only a few have large fanfiction followings. Why?
I believe fanfiction should stem from either dissatisfaction with the original story, or curiosity 'at the road not taken'.
Dissatisfaction is not dislike. Merely ideas you found unresolved, or counter to your understanding of the world (real or fictional). Some series are heavily weighted for their genre in terms of action, adventure or mystery. The aspect most people seem to want filled in is romance, to the point where whole slews of fics in a category are on nothing else but the romance.

What I look for here is a strong draw from the original source. Characters, Plot, and Setting are like Walls, Floor and the Furniture of a house and the Roof is your Story. You can't change too much without it becoming an entirely different building. If you go that far you would be better of just making an original work. Just because you use the same color paint on the walls(name of the characters), doesn't mean the house is the same.

On Pairings -
I've come to prefer stories without obvious romance. It often seems forced and quite often the story looses all sight of the original works plot, save the small percentage who further degrade the genres with the power of 'trwu luv' overcoming the obstacle, or being the strength of the hero. Don't get me wrong, I do think love a powerful motivator, however teenage hormones and lust seems to be many peoples qualifications of true love these days.
On Yaoi/Shonen Ai/Slash and Yuri/Femslash. - Number 1 get the term right. The Japanese terms have specific differences. Not to mention that Shoujo Ai is actually more broadly meant as romance than girl love. Some Slash is fine. Especially when it was in canon, or entirely the point of canon. Putting Slash where there wasn't any . . . be very careful and I might give it a chance, if you take it for granted that this pair of same gender will be a couple because 'I say so' without establishing any groundwork . . . no.
Further - keep in mind that not everyone can be gay. Some authors unintentionally end humanity by having all couple be same sex, they give no thought to future generations.
This leads to MPreg. No. Not possible. Not feasible. Not likely. No purpose that can't be solved with proper gender roles.
Gender-bending - tricky. If it is the only change sure no problem. (It is not a consideration if established as a part of canon.) No Way if it comes across as the only way to set up a pair of guys without them being homosexual, those stories usually dissolve into romance driven smut anyways.
On Harems. No.
Very well, there are the rare exceptions. There are stories where multiple spouses are established in canon, or inherent in the story's culture (say ancient China/Japan). I think I'd have less of an issue if there were more guy harems. Otherwise I'll let the males write what usually comes across to me as works of self-gratification.

In short, I prefer romance as canon based and realistic. In epic works it should be an afterthought. In short works, I enjoy those pieces that explore the situation, slice of time, reflection of development, or single complete scenes. If I see pairings in a summary, there's a good chance I won't read, even if I like the couple.

On Story Types -
Likes:
Stories that naturally diverge from a different interpretation of events. This should only be one, maybe two changes, and they should be reasoned or at least rationalized out.
Time Travel. Love it. Either physical transport or memory transplants. Prefer the unintentional to the 'let's go back and change everything' styles. Best if characters worry about changing things, or explore the paradox(es) of time travel. The mechanism should be inherent to canon or 'supernatural' in nature if the world is established as mundane.
Crossovers - Like them but I'm picky. These characters in that storyline - a merge - are all right but not a favorite. Character encounter in same universe (your basic Batman meets Superman, both exist in the same 'universe' but have separate stories) are okay when the universes can plausibly mesh. World jumping - single character(s), and a one way trip (or only one shot at return.) No commuting between canon-verses.
Some Sci-Fi, mostly fantasy/magic/middle ages, those that explore the mechanics of canon get bonus points. Things with a cultural or historical bent. Explorations of relationships of the non-romantic kind. Humor.
Dislikes:
Strait Romance, or Romance for Romance's sake.
'Real World' settings - with the exception of character and plot parallels.
American High School - unless that is canon. Respect other cultures and learn their education system. (Not that I want to read their teenage dramas much either.)

On writing style -
Grammar, Spelling, if you don't care, I don't. When in doubt, read aloud.
Humor is a difficult thing to write. You can't write jokes and pratfalls and expect laughs. Puns, a clever turn of phrase, word play, absurdity and timing, used carefully any of those can be funny.
Longer may not be better, but I enjoy a longer story, and story concepts tend to be fleshed out more.
Clarity is a must. Pronouns, events, concepts suffer when vague. Even in abstract pieces, your meaning should be clear, even if the subject is not.

There are always exceptions to my above criteria. Those works I tend to really like.

Please enjoy reading my works. If you have comments and questions, please be specific. And also be aware that I am horrible in correspondence.

May you never loose your love of reading,
