First and foremost...

I do not own any of the fandoms I write for. They belong to their respective creators, authors, companies, yada... Any similarities in my fanfiction to other works that are not mine, published or fanfiction, are unintentional.

Reviews and comments will be appreciated, suggestions on improvement and constructive criticism more so, but please don't be too harsh. I am currently taking it upon myself to improve my writing, and I'd like it very much if you could tell me what you think I could work on.

By the way, I'd prefer if you didn't judge me by my previous works, which in this case means anything posted before 2004. It's not that I'm thoroughly ashamed of them, but I wrote them quite a while ago and I don't think they're very good at all. I suppose I'm just keeping them up for old times' sake. My style of writing isn't really all that much different now, and probably not much better, but here's hoping it will become what I hope it to be.

Lastly, flame me if you must, but it is neither my fault nor problem if you hate my work, assuming it is only the difference of opinion you don't agree with, and frankly, it's stupid to insult someone because of it. Learn to be more open-minded. However, if you are going to flame me, at least type it legibly and if you really think my writing sucks, give me a valid reason to understand and tell me where I went wrong.

Bio:

Hi.

'Why such a weird name?' you might ask. 'S' means a lot to me, in it that not only does it start some of my various names - my real name is Sarah, and affectionately nicknamed S-chan by two of yours truly's friends - but it means so much more than just that. Then again, I'd rather not get into detail.

I was previously known as Satori Opaque, a name that did stick but I've now gotten tired of it. Heck, I may be prone to change my name again later on; you never know.

And of course, about me. I'll get straight to the point, since I know some people don't like reading ridiculously long-winded bios. I'm a teenage female in high school, Cantonese-Chinese, and probably too lazy for my own good. I like/love manga, anime, shounen-ai/yaoi, reading, writing, and music (and the list goes on). I dislike/hate prejudice in general, among many, many other things. I have obsessions. I go in and out of them. And that's about all that's important.

Previous:

Sorry to say, but I will not be continuing any YGO fanfiction in the near future. My obsessions, depending on which, fade with time, and this is no exception. I'll always remember it fondly as one of my most major obsessions and the fandom that got me into fanfiction, and I'm always a little sad when my obsessions leave, amongst other things, but hey, that's that.

I know my works don't say much about the extent of my obsession (I really did have a lot of ideas and I did do a lot of planning), but here's just telling you. Maybe if I'm lucky in the future, I'll be able to complete the fanfiction ideas I liked but never really got around to writing.

Hey, to the YGO fandom, along with the various favourite stories/authors... thanks, it was fun while it lasted. Don't get me wrong and certainly please don't be offended, I still think your works were great (otherwise I wouldn't have liked them in the first place), but everyone has to move on, right? My taste for YGO fanfiction has gone, but I'll still remember it.

And so, this leads us to...

Obsession:

...my current obsession. Okay, I don't really have an obsession right now, but just various favourite series. At the moment, my attention focuses around Naruto, Prince of Tennis (even though I've never seen an episode of the anime and read only the first chapter of the manga, and all my knowledge about it is through fansites and fanfiction), and GetBackers. I really only write for Naruto, though.

Website

My friend has an online RPG at which I'm helping out moderate at. Technically I don't own it, but how are we going to get going if we don't do some shameless self-promotion? If you're interested, why don't you drop by?

Hanrin Gakuen (also known as Half-Moon Academy), is a school like most others.

It has students, teachers, and... powerful secrets. This school educates both humans and demons, and its aim is to create harmony between the two races. The humans, of course, have no knowledge of this.

This will all be jeopardized when the principal brings a strange young man into the school.
