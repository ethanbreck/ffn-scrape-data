Bartleby's Reviewing Policy:

I've decided that there are two camps of authors on this site. The first camp is characterized by authors who simply enjoy expanding on the continuity of an already existing book, TV show, etc. This camp encompasses the majority of authors on this site. These authors tend to be much more eager to update their stories, but unfortunately they cut out many crucial revisions as a result. Reviewers from this camp are very quick to give praise before criticism. Supportive and encrouaging though these reviews are, they offer little guidance for the author.

The second camp joings the authors who use this site as a forum to receive criticism and insight on their work. These authors come in drastically fewer numbers. Authors of this camp spend a much greater time editing because they are concerned more with their own work than as it relates to the continuity. When reviewing, they remain supportive, but they offer more constructive criticism, offering suggestions to the author to help them improve their work instead of pushing them to write chapters as fast as they can. Members of these camps may overlap, but for the most part they remain divided in this way.

I am a member of the second camp, and, even if you're not, I will treat you as though you were when I review your story. I would like to think that every author at least wants to write the best fic they can, and I will try to give some advice or even just little pointers to help an author accomplish that goal. I tend to be very blunt with my reviews. If there's something I believe you need to work on, I will let you know. Please, if I do suggest something to you, do not be insulted by it. Know that I'm only trying to help, and I never intend to offend anyone by my critique. A good review for me balances support and criticism, and I'm still learning to find that balance, so bear with me while I'm still feeling my way out.

Thanks for reading this inordinately long public service announcement. Now, back to the fics...
