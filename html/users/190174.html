5/23/13

Hello! If you are taking time to read this, it's nice to meet you. I have been posting fics on ff.net for years and years now. I started with Harry Potter fics, all of which are old but One Last Hope is finished and not a bad read. Journey of Time has a lot of reviews, people seemed to like it, but it isn't finished. Most of the HP stuff was written before the final books came out so a lot of details are not canon.

Then there are my Supernatural and Vampire Diaries fics, not completed, nor will they ever be. They are entertaining to read but I can't remember where my plots were going very well and they were written during the first seasons of the shows. So much has changed since then! My sister and I are co-writing an Originals fic currently that really crosses over many fandoms but is based in the Vampire Diaries/Originals world. That should be coming out soon.

My Game of Thrones fics are some of my favorite. One is finished, yey! The other, Believe, has been one of my favorites to write but every time I go to sit and write the next chapter, nothing comes out. I'm stuck. I don't know if I'll ever get to finish it. I feel bad as I know a lot of people still like and favorite it. Maybe eventually that muse will come back around.

My current fic that I am having a blasty blast writing is my Star Trek fic. Anyone who knows me IRL knows I adore Chris Pine in everything he does and the new Star Trek movies have been phenomenal. I am a Trekkie at heart- I've watched almost all the episodes of all the different shows, but I especially love what JJ has done with these newer ones.

My favorite things to write are action/fighting scenes and realistic dialogue. Almost every story I write has some sort of kiss/romantic scene in the rain; I love rain scenes, just ask my sis. My favorite genres to write are sci fi and fantasy; I am currently in progress on an original novel in the fantasy genre. When I get stuck on what to do next writing that book, I go back to fanfiction to brush up on some of my skills and pull out some ideas.

My advice for all aspiring writers, fanfiction or otherwise- keep doing it. Keep writing. Read as much as you can, learn from it, and write every day. Get a blog and blog it. Get a notebook and write old school style. Just keep writing, keep practicing- it's the best way to get good at it.
