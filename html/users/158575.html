Hello!

I am currently a senior enrolled in highschool. I came across my love of writing by starting a fanfiction based upon Dragon Ball Z, upon where I invented an original villian. Since then, the original villian has become an entirely seperate entity unto himself, and I am currently endevoring to one day publish a fantasy series featuring the exploits of him and the characters that I and my co-author/illustrator/advisor/friend have created.

I have an avid love of reading, I highly suggest you check out the following book series if you like thrillers, fantasy, satire, or science fiction.

The Ender's and Bean series by Orsen Scott Card is a fabulously well-written series detailing the tale of a young child who has the weight of the world thrust unto his shoulders (both litterally and figuretively) at a young age.
The Discworld Series by Terry Pratchett. This series cannot even be begun to be described, it is so funny and chaotic. Suffice it to say, that I believe that this delightful series held the honor of being the best-selling series of novels before Harry Potter came along. Prattchet's portrayal of Death is wonderfully hillarious, and I find myself blazing through these novels with such speed that I fear the pages may be caught on fire from friction. I highly, HIGHLY, suggest that you check out this series. After all, how can one not love a series that takes place upon a disc-shaped world, which is supported upon the back of four mighty elephants whom stand on the back of a giant turtle that swims through space? I hightly reccomend the books Soul Music and Hogfather. In Soul Music, the tale of a teenage girl named Susan is told--only Susan isn't your average teenager, for Death is her grandfather. And when old granddad starts pondering the meaning of life after having to kill his adopted daughter and her husband who was once her apprentice, he begins to get depressed and starts slacking off in his duties, and thus is up to Susan to take up Death's sycthe and mount his horse, Binky,and fill in until Death can be found. plus the appearnce of a new form of wild music known as Music with Rocks In (Pratchett'sversion of Rock and Roll)appearing in the festering-jewel city of Ankh-Morpork doesn't help much. In Hogfather (which has nothing to do with the movie The Godfather, despite the similar titles) the Hogfather (Discworld's version of Santa Clause) has been killed and it's up to Death and his grouchy helper-elf Albert to fill in for the jolly man's shows until somehting can be done to solve the problem. I will forever fondly remember the chaos that ensues when Death takes over the place of Santa in a department store.

Department store manger: You can't give that child that, it's not safe.

Death: IT'S A SWORD, IT'S NOT MEANT TO BE SAFE.

Mother of Child: It's dangerous!

Death: IT'S EDUCATIONAL.

Mother: She could cut herself!

Death: THAT WILL BE AN IMPORTANT LESSON, THEN.

I love the Forgotten Realms series by R. A. Salvatore, and especially have a fondness for stories with the flamboyant drow Jarlaxle and the assassin Artemis Entreri.
Through Violet Eyes and its sequel With Red Hands is an little-known, underaprecciated thriller set in a world much like our own, the only difference is that in the world of this thriller, there exists individuals with violet eyes, who are the FBI's secret weapon against crime. The Violets can summon the souls of the dead into their bodies to testify at murder trials or figure out what went wrong witha plane crash or a head-on collision car accident. Except in Violet Eyes someone is murdering Violets, one by one, killing them off with the FBI helpless to stop him, until they put Violet Natalie Lindstrom into protective custody. With Red Hands is the grim sequel about a corrupt Viloet who is faking a testomy to allow a son to get away with his parent's murder, and the return from the dead of a gruesome serial killer, known as the Neddlepoint Killer (for the way he kills his victims)whom makes Hannibal Lector look like a Sunday school teacher

as for as far as my stories go, if I happen to stop writing for a long time, please, please give me a good kick in the arse with an e-mail telling me to get into gear, okay? You'd be doing me a favor.

I currently have plans brewing in my head for an epic fanfiction based in the Disworld, featuring Susan, Death, Rincewind, the Auditors, the History Monks, the City Watch, and about anyone else. But I might not start it for some time.
