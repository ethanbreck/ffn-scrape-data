November 14, 2014

So ... I've finished my Bachelor's degree and I've finally started writing again. I've been getting reviews asking if I'm going to finish stories or have them adopted and the truth is that I don't know. I haven't had any offers to adopt any of my stories although I'm not sure I'd be able to give one of them up. If I was going to finish them than I would probably gut them and remake them completely. Some of my stories have flaws and one of the reasons that I quit writing Wishful Thinking: The Baby even though I had two chapters finished was that I was convinced that there was a plot hole that I couldn't find. It might also be that I realized back then that I would need to rewrite Samantha Carter because I have a better understanding of depression and it wasn't presenting itself correctly through my writing.

I've started an Arrow/Batman AU crossover and I'm going to see where it goes before I start posting it. I might end up scrapping the entire story that I've written so far in favor of restarting it with an outline. For those familiar with my work I'm not a huge fan of dialogue moving a story forward but there will be dialogue in this fiction. In University I studied play writing and from that I'm taking the facts that sometimes the most important moment in a story isn't what's written but what actually happens in between the lines. Anyway if anyone is still reading my babbling I'm going to work to restart my writing in hopes that I find the passion and pride that I once felt from it. So please be patient and I hope that you enjoy my next offerings.

I am looking for a beta writer, mainly because I need someone to look for errors and plot holes so please PM me if you are interested. The person doesn't need to be an expert by any means but I would like someone who can spitball ideas for how much Alternate Universe is going into this story I'm thinking Oliver Queen won't be the Arrow but Bruce Wayne will be Batman, mainly because I can't imagine Bruce Wayne not being the Batman. I don't believe that someone with his intense emotional damage from his childhood could live a healthy life based on the theories of the days when the story was created. I'm not saying that it's impossible to be a emotionally healthy person if you have tragedy in your background but in the 1960s ect when Batman was created the idea of therapy and mental health was not very advanced and even someone with Bruce Wayne's money might not have gotten therapy because of the stigma of mental illness. This is only my opinion though so I'd prefer a Beta reader who had a similar take on Batman but it isn't required.

Lisa.
