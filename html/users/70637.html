Author has written 3 stories for Sailor Moon, Teen Titans, and Avatar: Last Airbender.

Views:

Fanfiction should be taken seriously sometimes. As an exercise, as I've found after many years, it has it's great merits of preparing a young/old artist for what lies ahead in more serious and real works of their own. If you can immate another artist's/writer's style of work and make it seem plausible and almost identical to the original style, then you're coming away with something. You're learning quality skills in dialouge, creativity and description, and still playing homage to an author you think is worthy by exploring their created world. There are exceptions to this, of course, by some fanfiction writers who completely change the characters and worlds.

But then we shouldn't really call those tales fanfiction, but instead, "trash".

Me:

I am a college student from the southeast portion of the US, majoring in art with a minor in computer science. All I ask as a member here is to be given good comments when the reader feels it's appropriate, and honest ones to as well.
