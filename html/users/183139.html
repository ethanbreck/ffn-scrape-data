The most important and most informative thing I could tell you about me and all the rest makes sense afterwards: I like starts.

Think about it for awhile and pretty much anything you can think of that has to do with that word, is probably true when it comes to me. Then add a few more that you haven't thought of, just assume there's more out there and you'll understand me so much better.

The other thing you should know: I get lost and confused by my own thoughts. It makes sense in my head but when I try to say so out loud it either comes out the clearest I could ever possibly hope for and it should have been recorded because I'll never remember how I said it again and it'll never come out in quite that perfect way ever again and because it's so complex but said so simply no one ever understands it fully because their thoughts were elsewhere or they weren't listening and I can't repeat it, I've just forgotten it because I've just seen soo deeply and for one brief shining moment I understand everything and there's peace in the universe again. Other times I get so wrapped up trying to find where that thought thread leads that I just wind up twisting it around myself and confusing myself so much that I wonder what I started with and can't find that end now either, or that thought it purposefully twisty and folds back in on itself and all it does is go in circles because truthfully the ends have welded together after I found the thread and it's a never ending loop, if I found an end to begin with, sometimes I just jump in at the middle.

Oh, 1 more: I like taking cliches and tweaking them just a little. They're still cliches but with just that smallest bit of freshness that makes them their own or in some cases, non-cliche stories, but really, in the end, isn't everything a cliche and has been done before? So why fight it? Why destroy the box when just a line or two outside of it can completely change the picture if you stand at the right distance, not too close and not too far.
