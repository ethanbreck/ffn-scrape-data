The basics:

Born '86, Swedish by birth, heritage and upbringing. Swedish is my first language, English is my second, followed by various languages I've studied at some point or other - Spanish, French, Latin, German, Ancient Greek and Japanese are the ones I've studied in school, and I have had very brief looks into Finnish and Dutch on my own. Oh, and I understand Danish and Norwegian well enough, because those languages are so closely related to Swedish. So hah.

...can you tell I'm a bit of a language freak?

Anyway, no stories here right now. I had a (quite silly and poorly done) crossover between Lord of the Rings and Monty Python and the Holy Grail earlier, but I didn't like it, and when the rule about no script-format fics came, I deleted it.

I'll probably eventually write something, but I'm a bit of a perfectionist, so I rarely post any of my ideas (thank heavens for that, or this profile'd be flooded with Mary Sues and generic stupidity).

I do like to read, though, and I try to review once in a while, not just showering said author with praise, but also coming with a bit of constructive criticism. I do try my best not to flame, though (and this is important, fan-authors. A flame is not the same as constructive criticism - if I point out any errors in your story, or give you advice for how to make it better, that's trying to help you. A flame is a review that goes personal, telling you that YOU suck, and not trying to help at all)... Of course, even the best of constructive reviews can hurt a bit, I know that. I know you put time and effort into making the story, and hearing it's anything but perfect is hard, but really, constructive criticism is there to make you improve as an author, so you can write stories that are enjoyable for everybody.

My main fandoms are Lord of the Rings, Harry Potter, the Discworld, the Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy andthe Chronicles of Narnia (Notice a pattern? I'll give you a hint: think "nationality of the author"). I tend to read pretty much anything I get my hands on, though, so it's not too unlikely I'll wander into some other fandom once in a while. As for movies - I don't watch much tv or movies, to be honest, but the ones I like, I watch repeatedly.

On a final note, my nickname. It's quite a long (and at the same time a very short) history. About three years ago, I was making a character for a Hogwarts game, and needed a last name for her. A site I was just stumbling across had a LotR elven name generator, and I got "Gayahithwen" out of it. I used the nick, first for that Hogwarts game, and later on as my nickname when I entered the LotR fandom more fully (still before the movies came out). Eventually I became aware that it was a bit of a mix - ancient elvish root combined with Sindarin, and an ending I'm not quite sure about - but by that time, I was already known under this name, and it'd be so much work to get used toa new nickname. If the mix is too much for you, you can just call me "Gaya", though. It's what most people call me, after all, and it prevents people from misspelling my nick. ^_~

Oh, and on a final final note, if you find any errors in my language, please excuse me, and after thatpoint them out to me. I'm not perfect, after all, and it's possible I've made some in this presentation (after all, it's mostly just a long ramble about me). English is, as mentioned, not my first language, but that's no reason to abuse it, and I do want to avoid that.

Alright, ramble-- err, presentation finished, please continue as normal.
