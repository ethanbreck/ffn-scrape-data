❝ to you who has no name, with love from the end of this earth. ❞

In the year of 2003 I discontinued my fanfiction. That particular reason being that FF.net decided to delete users' fanfiction without any prior notice. Sadly, those stories of which that were written... were lost forever. Not backed up, to say the least. Nevertheless, I became disappointed with this site and left with my friends, amongst other authors who were equally as upset. I've returned now. My previous works will still be up, but they are discontinued.

❝ so here is something I have to say that I can't stand. if you don't like it, then don't read it. ❞

Now, saying this I hope to not offend, but since I feel as though I'll be coming back to this site after a super long hiatus... I just have to get this off my chest! Y'know when people start writing fanfiction, original work, what-have-you and their characters are just so... Mary Sue? Well, here's how you will know you are reading or writing one (this is me just making an assumption, cos I avoid writing Mary Sue. Other people can, but I won't. I mean, wry else do I mainly RP boys?) If your character starts doing things horribly OOC and begins to do things YOU want to do but can't, then that's a red flag right there. Any odd quirks that you might have will most likely be even more exaggerated in your Mary Sue character. When things in your life are boring and you are rike, "FML. FOREAL" then you can expect your Mary Sue character to have it even worse to the point of being super ridiculous (hey, I'm all for character torture, but some things are really unlikely to happen. Like multiple rapes, abortions, suicide attempts, etc.) Yes, I know some characters are pretty cliche in some video-game, movie, TV show, novel franchises and you are forced into making an original identity for them. But when doing so, stray it from being reflective of yourself. It will save readers from going through a trippy, emotional roller-coaster ride (probably thinking your character is bipolar or some shat rike that) So, as an author, if you notice any of this behaviour in my work (my newer ones)... FAHKIN' TELL ME, DUDE. So I don't seem rike a d-bag and fall into a tumultuous cyclone of depression and angst.

❝ life is short. break the rules, forgive quickly, kiss slowly, love truly, laugh uncontrollably. and never regret anything that made you smile. ❞

I should write something a little bit more about me? Let's see, if you were here back in the early years of FF.Net, my penname would've been "AngelWingStarX" or "Yot". The first fanfiction fandom I've ever written for was Dead or Alive and then I progressively moved towards Dynasty Warriors. Although I still do enjoy the two, I'd really like to take a stab at the Tekken and Resident Evil fandoms, for sure. I have enjoyed writing in advance literate roleplays, as of late, and would like to hone my writing skills into making a series, or two. I'd like to rewrite some old stories, but I can't seem to delete them and start over (call it emotional attachment, sad I know) So, currently, those series will be discontinued. I'm also an avid reader, writer, and learner. Throw in some spontaneous OCD perfectionism in there, too. If I have a characterization or fact wrong, then please point it out nicely in my comments instead of slaughtering off my head. I also like to use a lot of em-dashes instead of semi-colons, because semi-colons looks funny to me. I ALSO RIKE TO TYPE IN CAPS LOCK AND ENGRISH. huu ~ 8D
