I am STARSCREAM of the Decepticons!

I decided to return seeing that the so-called purge has ended and turned out to be a big load of nothing.

I tend to write crossovers, instead of straight in-universe works, I might try hand at in-universe at later time I got something in the works. Anyway, some years ago I drew up some rules of how I write crossovers due to some more than spectacular failures on my part. Here are my rules:

When I formulate x-over, the ratio is 90% based in logic about 10% cool factor. Here is my thought process:
1. I postulate a "What-If" where I play it out in my mind of how the two or more universes mesh together
2. I ask myself "Will it work"
--A. Yes. Proceed to step 3
--B. No. Scrap it
3. Break down the universes into their most basic components throw out what doesn't work or meld, keep what will

4. When merging characters from one universe with another try to create backstories for those characters that would make it logical for them to exist in the other universe
5. Create a story outline of how you want the series to proceed and where you want it to go
6. The last step begin writing

Now here are some standing orders I developed when I write crossovers because it stems from either seeing certain conventions in comic books and TV.
1. Try to avoid time travel if and when possible.
2. Dimensional portals and such

The reasons is they're so cliched its not even funny and for me because when such conventions employed the crossover almost seemed forced and almost formulatic.

Starscream's tips of fanfiction writing:

These are what I've developed over the years based various indiscretions on my part as a writer

1. Self-Inserts. There are some who can pull it off with great effect. But self inserts (or mary/gary-sues) should be avoid at all costs since all objectivity goes right down the toilet.

2. Manuscripts. As a general rule I don't go straight to my computer and start typing (unless it's a generally short story and the idea is fresh in my mind.) I always write out a handwritten manuscripts and make changes when necessary as I'm typing.

3. Outline. For most of my fics, especially if is a series following a specific storyline, I tend to a outline where I want to go with the series such as my ExoSquad/Gundam xover

4. Research. Never start a fic bindly, always do the proper research and development.
