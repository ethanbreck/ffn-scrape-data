Author has written 6 stories for Harry Potter, and Mass Effect.
Special Note: I'm a "him" not a "her." :P

Stories (note that these are all connected, it's a series, my "books" 5 to 7):

Dursley Dream: What would happen if the Dursleys got sucked into Harry's world? Would he find peace? A maddening collection of dreams Harry confuses for reality haunts him awake and asleep and the faint glitter on his skin bothers him even more. Follow Harry through the wretched dreams, feeling just as confused as he is.

Mark of Ancients: The glitter is back and now Harry knows how to use it. It's causing quite a bit more commotion as he gets plunged into a series of secrets, lies and negligence the likes of which he has no idea.

Book of Memories: Harry's state after another meeting with Voldemort in Mark of Ancients leaves him in a situation far worse than ever before... There's something in the school itself that Voldemort wants and Harry wants, but both far more than they know at first...

Staff of Cybele: If there's any reason to read this, it's to meet the Staff of Cybele itself, otherwise known as Raides. A haunting dream, a shock of emotion, a close death. All fingers point at Harry but he's innocent, being plagued again by the haunting dreams and the ravenous mood swings from the mark of ancients.

A Bit About Me:

I write these stories out of a pure love for Harry Potter. I was spellbound after seeing Sorcerer's Stone just once and then went to get the books the next day. Finishing Goblet of Fire left me somewhat empty with a big block of inspiration in my head waiting to get released and that's what started Dursley Dream and the rest of the series.

For those interested, Book of Memories reflects a part of my own life and certain parts of it are much more personal than it appears at first. I'll tell you I had no problems writing scenes between Harry and Cho in it... Raides is a sort of personification of me, aside from the fact that she's not my species.

Do take note that from my first story to the last I've written, my writing in general has gotten better, from realism to style to content and plot/theme. I know some people find the first, Dursley Dream, hard to read.

If you find my writing interesting, I've got a story on www.fictionpress.net that's a completely original world, with stuff from my childhood daydreaming (which is probably more fantastical than you think at first) mixed, swirled, tossed and beaten entitled Before the Dawn.
