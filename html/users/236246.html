Profile Update: September 23, 2006

One week ago, I told you I would try.

I started something. Seriously. I bought awesome fingerless gloves, an inspiration to me as I feel like a true writer, and I have coffe sitting next to me. I'm feeling fabulous from my first garbage plate (a total experience, BTW), and ready to finish what I started.

I can't explain to you why I want to finish all of a sudden. I've almost given up, but recent events that even I can't place has made me jump into the past and try what I've given up. I want to finish, and I almost want to restart what I've left behind. I almost miss this place. It's a safe haven for writers, and it lets them explore what they truly want to try. It's a great place to be.

So maybe in another week (depending on how much I want to study for my psych test, or how much I want to write my religion paper) I'll have something posted. I'm basing that number on my abilities in the past to get stuff done. Hopefully it works. I'm truly inspired.

I've changed a lot since becoming a member of this website. I'm a tentative '09 grad about 3 and a half hours away from home. I grew up, and know limits to some things, and am learning what my limitations are for other things. I've become a different person, and I'm proud of who I've become. I'm not going to deny the impact this site had on me. It was a part of my daily routine for a year or two or three, and I 'met' some nice people here. People were nice to me, and people were not so nice. I also grew a quite large imagination, something I don't need when trying to study neurons and reflexes! But since pulling myself away from this site, I've changed a little. I found internet sites that connect me to my own peers (IE facebook. Bad, bad addiction). Change is a part of life, and I realized that even when I was young. Quite a few of my stories involved change. It's such a big part of life, in my opinion, that I acknowledge it. I don't like it, but I deal with it, because it helped me to flourish into the beautiful person I am today.

So my treat to you, as a part of my change in life, is the stories below. I'm not the greatest writer, but the stories below reflect part of the change I experienced when going through the teen years. I've got the biggest imagination, and ideas that blind me from grammar and proper sentence structure. But that's me. It'll always be me.

So deal. Ok?

What you heard was a dream and nothing more . . .
