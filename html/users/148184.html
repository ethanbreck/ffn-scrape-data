My name's really Danielle--Diana is my Craft name, and 'Le Defonceur' is (supposed to be) 'The Digger' in French. I figured 'Digger' is appropriate since I'm now living my post-graduation dream of being an archaeologist. A fair warning: golden treasures and untold riches it ain't, people. On the other hand, it ain't half-nekkid women either, as Indiana Jones would have you believe. Hal-lay-loo-yah for that, I suppose ~8 Anyhoo, it's still a blast, though it does seem to keep me from my fanfiction duties... :Laughs Nervously: Heh. Heh.

My fav fics bounce from being Harry Potter or LOTR one week (yeah, y'all saw that one coming) and used to be that I'd spend hours over in the MST3K section of 'TV Land', but I won't get into THAT right now...anyone looking for a good mistie of a bad fic can find them here, at Web Site Number 9: . It is, quite simply, Mistie-rific.

Nothing else much here has changed, and it still holds true that any flamers will be taken care of by my trusty water hose, Mr. Spray, who I've recently affixed with a nifty new state-of-the-art nozzle, endowing it with the ability to both spray and now spit water at those who deem my stories worthy of a good dissing. Opinions are always welcome, but nasty comments and other such evilness will only make me want to get Medieval on your pansy ass, fool : Sorry. It seems that since I've moved back home I've been unconciously channeling the spirit of Mr. T, which is particularly odd considering he's not dead yet. And now that I think about it, I doubt if he were dead that anybody would tell him, because he'd probably get hella wicked crazy mad on your ass, anyhow, deceased or not. Hmmph. Foo'.

Anyone interested in the paranormal and such things that are going to be featured in my stories (alright, story), feel free to email me with any questions. I've been studying the supernatural and such for the past 15 years or so, and would like to think that one day I'll become an expert in it. Hell, I'm only 24. I gotta have SOMETHING to look forward to in life. What else can I say? While my friends were raised on "The Wonder Years" and "Life Goes On," I was brought up on "Unsolved Mysteries" and "Scooby-Doo." Actually, it was a LOT of Scooby-Doo. Oh, what a fascinating childhood I had...nothing says I'm still not trying to live in it, though :) Quick! To the Wayback Machine!
