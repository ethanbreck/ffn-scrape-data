BTW, I am officially STORMWRECK fan # 1 (that is, I am the No. 1 supporter in Storm from "X-Men Evolution" and Shipwreck from "G.I. Joe" pairings! Anyone who says otherwise gets a size-10 shoe coming their way! Why do I support this pairing? (besides the obvious reason that I'm insane?) Look at the evidence:
1.) Opposites attract; Shipwreck is caring but reckless, hedonistic, brash, crude, and completely oblivious at times. Storm is gentle, refined, elegant, and cultured. In other words, it's a perfect fit!
2.) Storm has already tried dating Dracula, and has tried to start something with Hank, Forge and Warren and none of them have really connected to her in that special way. Shipwreck, on the other hand, has really been there for her when she was in serious need. He comforted her when her nephew Evan was charged with murder, he tried to help her go through her grieving process and didn't try to take advantage of her when she was falling apart, and he actually saved her life when Spears tried to throw Power-8 (a poison to mutants) at Storm. Thus, it is apparent that Shipwreck, despite his many flaws, is a very dedicated friend who sincerely cares about Storm's safety and well-being rather than just "a roll in the hay".
3.) Shipwreck is loyal and faithful to who he cares about. It was hinted by Red Witch that before his wife, Mara, left him for another man who was richer, and abandoned her mutant children, Shipwreck was a dedicated husband.
4.) Shipwreck cares about peace between mutants and humans, like the X-Men do. Unlike bigots such as Stryker and Graydon Creed, Shipwreck refused to leave his family when his five children were all found out to be mutants, and he went through the thick and thin to ensure that he would always be there for them, that they would be safe. He also adopted an additional mutant child, Beak, to save him from being a circus freakshow at a bar in Europe. It takes a strong man to be willing to stand by his children during the war between mutants and humans.
5.) Shipwreck also deeply cares about the X-Men and Misfits as well. He tries to be there for other people outside his family (like when he drove away Tabitha's father when he tried to harrass her in "Another Week Of Random Madness"), even if the X-Men don't appreciate it. Storm has yet to show the same behavior towards the Misfits (OK, they drive her nuts, but still...)
6.) Storm does acknowledge that Shipwreck does come through for her; she even thanked him in the fanfics, "This Soap Opera Called Life" and "A Walk With Shipwreck".
7.) Storm has never outrightly told Shipwreck that she hated him. She has tried to electrocute Shipwreck with her lightning, said he drove her crazy, wished he'd go away, given him more vicious beatings that the cops did to Rodney King, but Storm never once said, "I hate you". (Edit: OK, she recently said it in Chapter 56 of "Evolution XMJ" but considering that it was when the Shipwreck clones from Mojo's dimension terrorized the X-Mansion, I'm willing to say that "I hate you" was purely circumstantial)
8.) They say the more a couple fights, the happier they will be. Need I say more?
9.) Shipwreck has never given up on Storm despite all the insults and electrocutions she had thrown at him. He'd always come back with a smile on his face, like Steve Urkel on his quest for Laura in the TV sitcom, "Family Matters". And if you recall at the end of the series, Steve and Laura actually married because Laura finally admitted that deep down, she couldn't picture her life without Steve, despite his annoying qualities. Can anyone say "future marriage"?
10.) Shipwreck may have his problems and flaws, but Storm should realize that no one is perfect, and what's troubling is that she's hoping to find someone who's the perfect suitor, her fantasy "Prince Charming", if you will. Well, Shipwreck may not be a "Prince Charming", but he is certainly the underprivelged knight in rusty armor who never gives up, they lowest guy on the totem pole everyone cheers for, the loser who just never quits.
11.) 'Cause I say so! And may I speak softly, but I carry a BIG mallet, so don't piss me off!
