My Reading and Reviewing Policy

I have three very simple rules:
1) I don't ask you to read
2) I don't ask you to review
3) Take responsibilty for what you say

What it all comes down to is this: I'm not holding a gun to your head, so you don't have to feel obligated to do anything. If you do read one of my stories, and you do feel the need to review (one way or another), have the balls to take responsibility for what you say. Either log in and use your account or don't log in and leave an email address. Mind you, if you're saying something nice, it won't bother me too much if you don't (there's really nothing to take responsibility for there).

I have a theory when it comes to reviewing: Don't waste your time. That doesn't mean that I don't review. It means that I review authors and stories that make me want to review. Sometimes that means being a little harsh on people I can tell aren't living up to their potential, sometimes it means just telling an author that I'm still reading.

The whole theory comes from something one of my English teachers mentioned in reference to theatre. "You have every right to sit on your hands." What he meant was that if you thought it was horrible, you didn't have to clap. If you thought it didn't deserve a standing ovation (even if everyone else was standing), you could remain seated. If I don't think something is worth my time, I don't bother reviewing...especially since reading my review will be wasting someone else's time.
