On Books
I also love stories that have to do with the supernatural. Thus it is not unexpected that I own all of the Anita Blake series by Laurel K. Hamilton (highly recommended peeps! Got my friend Qui Gon hooked!). Though I hate nearly all romance novels. I still love cartoons. I'm a mythology freak. I occasionally read folk stories. Love Sherlock Holmes.


Usually I shy away from fantasy books. They do weird things to my head.

'There's so many', I think to myself, 'If I pick the first one up I'll want the next fifteen in the series.' And I know that there are that many b/c they are sitting right there, staring at me...taunting me. I can practically hear them, Buy us. You know you want to and you will. Go ahead read the first one of us, you'll come back for the rest of us...everyone does and you're no different. All anyone wants is to lose themselves in our pages and there's more than enough of us to go around. Come on baby, buy us. We'll take such good care of you. 'Why are the books talking to me? And in such a creepy voice!' Thus, I end up returning the first book to its brethren on the shelf and unconsciously wipe my hand on my jeans before I move on without a backward glace.
I have a strange obsession with languages and dictionaries though I can't spell for beans in english, french or japanese.

Anne Rice's vampire novels never did anything for me though I did enjoy the movies. I love Jim Butcher's Dresden Files books (yes, I also got Quo Gon addicted to this series. Also highly recommended!). Nicolas Cage wanted to make it a movie and series (for sci-fi channel) but so far I've only seen the series and I gotta say that since I've read the books I was expecting so much more--it was okay.

PROGRESSION OF MY STORIES (WORKS IN PROGRESS)

All Stories are on HIATUS until I locate them!!

Yes, I lost them all...I blame my pc with its "deteriorating" hard drive--whatever stories I did retrieve were not in the right folders and some are half "corupted" and my sister threw out my notebooks!! She thought it was left over junk from school--like old homework assignments.

Just thought that some peeps would like to know where I am in these. I had to put most of my stories on hold but that does not mean that I have given up on any of them and nor do I intend to 'cuz I do plan on finishing all the ones that I post up here. The situation over here has improved enough that I feel confident that I can continue on with these stories and be able to do them the justice that they so rightfully deserve which I do not believe I would have been able to do this past year. I have come up with some new stories as well and as soon as I have them written to at least two chapters I will post 'em here, some are originals so I am debating--arguing--with myself whether or not to even post 'em on the internet or to change them into fan fictions 'cuz that's easily done.

Let it be known that I probably have a mild case of ADD--ya know, that attention disorder-mabober thingy?-- or a major case of it depending on who you ask, which is a real pain in the butt 'cuz it causes me to lose intrest in something fairly quickly resulting in my jumping around a lot in the things that I do. For example, my stories: I have tons that have only a chapter or two, paintings that are half way done, songs left unsung--damn, now I'm starting to rhyme: ton, done, unsung. But seriously, I'm even switching between more windows than I care to admit while doing this and its too damned early for this.

Usually I take at least two of my stories everywhere I go or if I forget I at least have a note book so that I can write down whatever may pop up in my head. So I have a ton of notes and books full of writings and some times I cannot find the right book so I either just write--continue the story on another paper or type on my pc or laptop. You know what that equals to? Two or up to four different versions of one story. And I have to read them all again then either combine, drop, or add to it to make that now finished single story that gets posted. A question: How many directions can one story take? My answer: Way, way too many.

"And what have we learned today, children?" "1S&A1 basic story ideatime= 1 very sleep deprived S&A with massive headache & 5 different versions of 1story" "Very good, children."

Yes, I consider ten chapters to be lengthy for an internet story that is an average of two thousand words per chapter and I think that a short story that is posted on the internet should be no more than five chapters long but of course it might very depending on how many words per chapter. I don't have a words per chapter quota that I must reach before I end it and begin a new chapter. I end it when it feels as if it is done, at a close, when I can no longer improve upon it, or if it just seems like its a good spot to finish at--as in a cliffhanger or for suspense.
