I've read a lot of fics, and I've come to the conclusion that the success of a story lies in three things: pacing, narration, and grammar. If you story is rushed or drags on, it's not pleasurable to read. If it feels contrived, or the plot is weak, or there are plot holes you could drive a truck through--your fic isn't going to fly. Furthermore, if your spelling is sloppy and you ignore the rules of syntax, I don't know why you bother. You're here to write, aren't you? So learn your language properly. Watch out for logic holes and discontinuity errors: if your character was limping two paragraphs ago, she can't run now. If your character is a soldier, learn about protocol and rank and military procedures. Be as realistic as you can within the boundaries of the world you're writing in. Don't create AUs and mangle the original characters half to death; in which case you can just slap new names on them and take it to fictionpress, because it sure as hell isn't fanfiction anymore.

Bonus points for style: if you can make your reader laugh or smile or shiver or gasp, you're definitely getting somewhere. AUs are harder than other fics, despite what you think, but you can pull it off if you think it through. Background details are also lovely: if your story is set in a royal court, the richer the political scene, the better and more believable your story. Give me things I wouldn't have thought of myself. Do something unexpected and ambitious. Enjoy writing!
