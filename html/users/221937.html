I feel like I should add a note about my head canon for Charles Carson after the events of the final episode (apologies if you've not seen it yet, SPOILERS, but then you probably won't be reading anything I write until you've seen it!).

I've not been feeling much like writing since it aired because I didn't quite know how to deal with the shaking. It's not explained what it is, exactly, but I felt like the natural assumption was that it would end up being Parkinson's and I don't want to go down that route. I refuse to believe that a beautiful expressive man like Charles Carson could be reduced to that, although I know that such is the way of life at its most unfair.

However, I've just read a chapter of a fic by Olehistorian, who suggests that Carson has Essential Tremor, where the tremor occurs when the hands are in use, but not in rest. Though hands (and other extremities) can be affected, it does not prevent the person from leading a full and happy life. Olehistorian makes reference to Katharine Hepburn, who I confess ran through my mind when the subject came up on the show.

So, I don't know how much or how often I will dwell on the shaking, but if and when I do, this will be the diagnosis I use.

On a lighter note, although Elsie called him Charlie right at the end, I believe she only does so in exceptionally tender moments, or in complete privacy. This too will be reflected in anything I write from now on.
