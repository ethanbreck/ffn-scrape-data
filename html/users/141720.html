I am Icy, wear a bra, and stuck in between wanting to grow up and being a kid.

Misc: Not new to ff.net. When authors date their profile, I'm happy. I am guilty of being a lurker. I rarely review (sorry). I rant... a lot. I like building stories around strange dialogues. I tend to put pieces of real life in all the characters that I write about.

I have a fondness for good entertainments because they breed the best plot bunnies. Phenomenal books and movies are often just for sweet pleasure and respected appreciation. They should not be tainted by my own shortcomings. Bad entertainments just make you want to start from scratch. But I will always have a strange fancy for trashy romance novels; they satisfy my inner hopeless romantic. I also have a special place in my heart for a good anime among them are "Cowboy Behop" and "His and Her Circumstances".

My fondness for Harry Potter stops at the impressively elaborate world that J.K. Rowling created. I am not impressed with her writing style although I can do no better. I am not impressed with her characterization; she can definitely a thing or two from Jeffery Chaucer. The characters are far too black and white for my taste. Everyone is two-dimensional and fits nicely in all their stereotypes which is the main reason why I like Draco Malfoy, Severus Snape, and Hermione Granger. I think they are the closest things to actual three-dimensional people. And yes I think I think J.K. Rowling is overrated.

After Harry Potter and the Half-Blooded Prince, Malfoy and Snape rise from my character list and became my favorites. I have a thing for characters with flaws. I hope desperately that the seventh book will not kill hope for Malfoy's redemption. Because I think it is possible. I am also fasincated with Hermione Granger despite her two-dimensional portrayal in the books. I think she also has great potential to develop into a vivid character with ups and downs, flaws and merits.

I do not like Ron. I do not like Ginny. I am happy with the rest of the Wealsey family except for Percy for obvious reasons. I absolutely despise Ron and Hermione together. I think they are doomed to failure in the beginning. Ginny and Harry are so obvious that it makes me gag.

In short, my list of top characters in no particular order: Hermione, Luna (she is so eccentric that it warms my heart in the oddest ways), Malfoy, and Snape.

Truthfully, I am fascinated with the world the JK Rowling has built but I find her rather stereotypical good vs. bad story and two dimensional characters dull. But kudos to her for letting us to run wild with her characters.

Throw your cautions to the wind, take a chance, and read my work. Constructive criticisms will be weclomed and endured. Unwarranted flames will be puffed away. Compliments will be thanked.

Alwayz,
Icy
